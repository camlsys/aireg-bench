Academic Compliance Monitor employs a hybrid model architecture combining Random Forest classifiers and sequence-based RNNs to analyze behavioral patterns derived from time-stamped input streams such as keyboard dynamics and environmental audio cues within examination halls. Targeting educational institutions, this system aims to detect outlier behaviors indicative of cheating or unauthorized assistance by synthesizing tabular event data with temporal activity sequences. Its technological capacity lies in effective anomaly detection over multimodal but non-biometric input to minimize false positives and enable prompt alerts for exam supervisors. The system is provided by Veritas Learning Systems, a company specializing in compliance and integrity tools for regulated educational environments. The directly affected AI subjects are students taking monitored exams, whose conduct is analyzed to maintain institutional standards of academic honesty.
