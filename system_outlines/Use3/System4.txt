Learning Outcome Navigator employing support vector machines (SVM) optimized for classification of learner performance categories from standardized test scores and questionnaire responses within higher education institutions and professional development programs. The system’s primary purpose is to identify learners at risk of underperformance and recommend targeted interventions by efficiently categorizing achievement levels across multiple disciplines. Its capabilities stem from the SVM’s robustness in classification tasks with small to medium-sized structured datasets, facilitating scalable and interpretable performance stratification. Developed by Novus Educational Services, a consulting and technology firm delivering AI-driven diagnostic tools for academic success management. Directly affected AI subjects are students monitored through assessment outcomes and academic advisors or program coordinators using the system to guide learner support decisions.
