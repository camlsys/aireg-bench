Critical Incident Prioritization Framework utilizes Support Vector Machines (SVM) trained on structured emergency call metadata combined with a Transformer-based large language model (LLM) fine-tuned for emergency communication transcripts to determine the priority ranking of incidents for first responders. It targets emergency dispatch units within police, firefighting, and emergency medical services sectors aiming to improve decision consistency and reduce human bias in urgent resource distribution. By combining precise classification with contextual understanding of caller information, the system supports rapid, reliable triage of incoming requests under high-pressure scenarios. The system is developed by Veritas Emergency Systems, an enterprise specializing in AI solutions that enhance operational efficiency in crisis management. The persons affected include dispatch decision-makers, first responders, and individuals requesting emergency aid.
