Guardian Signal Controller uses a combination of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) and Random Forest classifiers to analyze live video feeds and sensor measurements at intersections. The system serves municipal traffic signal control units aiming to optimize signal timing while preventing unsafe traffic conditions such as red-light violations and pedestrian conflicts. Its core function is to detect traffic anomalies and predict accidents at intersections by processing and interpreting visual and sensor inputs, with CNNs extracting spatial features from video and Random Forests making robust classification decisions under uncertain conditions. It is provided by Aegis Mobility Technologies, a firm dedicated to smart infrastructure safety solutions. The directly affected users are road users including drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians interacting with traffic signals governed by the system.
