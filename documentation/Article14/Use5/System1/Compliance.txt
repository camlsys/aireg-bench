**Article 14**

### Ensuring Effective Human Oversight During Use

The Talent Insight Model (TIM) has been architected with integrated human-machine interface (HMI) components to enable effective natural person oversight throughout operational deployment. The system incorporates an interactive dashboard providing real-time transparency into scoring metrics and decision rationales for each candidate-job match. This dashboard includes visualizations of key feature activations, confidence intervals on relevance scoring, and anomaly alerts triggered by statistical deviations from training set distributions. These elements empower human overseers—typically recruitment professionals—to monitor system outputs dynamically, facilitating early detection of irregular behavior or erroneous pattern recognition during resume parsing and ranking. The system’s modular design maintains responsiveness and clear state reporting to support continuous human monitoring, including pausing and review functionalities, thereby fostering sustained situational awareness throughout the recruitment cycle.

### Risk Mitigation Through Oversight Measures

Human oversight mechanisms are embedded to mitigate risks linked to inaccurate candidate evaluations, potential discrimination, and privacy infringements that could impact health, safety, or fundamental rights. The system’s underlying model was fine-tuned on a dataset of over 1 million anonymized resumes and 250,000 validated job descriptions balanced across diverse demographics and roles to minimize bias. Dedicated bias detection modules operate continuously, flagging model outputs where demographic parity gaps exceed defined statistical thresholds. Oversight tools alert recruiters when flagged outputs arise, prompting manual review prior to decision finalization. Additionally, the system includes a bias correction feedback loop, enabling human-in-the-loop retraining triggers when systemic issues are detected. Together, these measures help ensure that residual risks after automatic bias mitigation are addressed proactively through informed human intervention aligned with reasonably foreseeable misuse scenarios.

### Proportionate Oversight Measures Tailored to System Autonomy

Given the semi-automated nature and medium autonomy level of the Talent Insight Model, provider-implemented measures focus on integrated technical controls complemented by comprehensive user guidance for deployers. Provider-built features include an embedded override function (“stop” button) allowing recruiters to halt automated filtering at any stage, ensuring manual control over candidate shortlisting decisions. The model’s confidence scoring thresholds are adjustable at deployment to reflect organizational risk tolerance and operational context, enabling tailored sensitivity to false positives or negatives. Provider-delivered documentation includes detailed human oversight protocols, risk scenarios, and interface usage guidelines to assist deployers in implementing effective monitoring and manual validation coincident with their recruitment workflows. These combined measures constitute a layered oversight approach calibrated to the system’s operational complexity and real-world use environments.

### Enabling Natural Persons to Understand, Monitor, and Control the System

The Talent Insight Model is supplied with comprehensive onboarding materials and in-application interpretability tools to support natural persons assigned to human oversight roles. The system’s output explanation module uses state-of-the-art attention heatmaps and feature importance scores, helping users accurately interpret the basis of candidate-job match recommendations. Training modules emphasize awareness of automation bias risks, reinforced by periodic system notifications reminding users not to over-rely on AI-generated rankings without supplementary qualitative judgment. The user interface allows direct intervention capabilities, including manual candidate scoring adjustments, exclusion of certain candidate profiles, and invocation of full manual filtering bypass when necessary. An emergency stop feature is accessible at all times through a dedicated interface button that safely halts automated processing while maintaining data integrity. 

Records of processing activities comply with applicable data protection regulations, with specific logs detailing instances where processing of special categories of personal data (such as disability status, gender, or ethnicity included only when strictly necessary for bias detection and correction) is conducted. These logs justify the necessity of processing these data types for ensuring fairness and document the absence of viable alternatives, facilitating external auditability and accountability for oversight activities involving sensitive information. This layered transparency and control framework equips human overseers with the tools and information necessary for nuanced evaluation and intervention throughout the AI-assisted recruitment process.
