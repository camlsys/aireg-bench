**Article 14**

**Design for Effective Human Oversight**

Priority Response Analytics integrates human-machine interface tools to facilitate effective human oversight throughout system operation. The user interface deployed in emergency dispatch centers provides a real-time, dynamic visualization of incident prioritizations, combining confidence scores from the Gradient Boosted Decision Tree (GBDT) model and the Transformer encoder’s natural language analysis. Dispatch operators interact with a dashboard that clearly delineates input sources, model rationale (highlighting key textual features and data signals), and priority level categorizations with associated uncertainty measures. This setup allows operators to maintain continuous situational awareness and intervene or query the system promptly if anomalous or unexpected outputs occur. An iterative human-in-the-loop design process, involving emergency dispatchers and domain experts over six development cycles, validated usability and ensured that oversight mechanisms align with operational practices under time-critical constraints.

**Mitigation of Risks to Health, Safety, and Fundamental Rights**

Human oversight protocols are explicitly structured to reduce risks related to the misprioritization of emergency incidents, which might lead to delayed responses or improper resource allocation. The oversight functionalities emphasize quick anomaly detection and ready escalation paths, addressing errors that could arise despite robust model training or fail-safes. Realistic misuse scenarios evaluated in pre-market risk assessments included atypical input patterns (e.g., obfuscated or partial dispatch notes) and model output inconsistencies under data drift conditions. To address these, the system incorporates confidence interval thresholds triggering flags to alert human operators of uncertain or borderline predictions. These workflows empower operators to verify or override model outputs, thereby preventing harm or rights infringements that could result from automated misclassifications or delays.

**Calibration of Oversight Based on Risk and Autonomy Levels**

Given the system’s deployment in high-stakes, real-time emergency response contexts, oversight measures are proportional to its high-risk nature and partial autonomy in decision support. Before market placement, Sentinel Technologies embedded core oversight mechanisms (Article 14(3)(a)) such as transparent output explanations, interactive override controls, and automated anomaly detection modules within the software. These internal features are supplemented by recommended operational protocols (Article 14(3)(b)) communicated to deployers, including mandatory dispatcher training programs on system capabilities and limitations, standard operating procedures for monitoring alert flags, and incident logging for audit trails. The degree of autonomy—advisory prioritization rather than autonomous dispatching—was a deliberate design decision to keep humans centrally responsible and maintain direct intervention capacity.

**Enabling Comprehension and Control Over the AI System**

To empower human overseers for informed monitoring and intervention (Article 14(4)), the system delivers comprehensive explanatory interfaces that clarify both the strengths and limitations of Priority Response Analytics. Dispatchers receive contextual explanations of model outputs, including key features influencing priority scores and associated confidence metrics derived from ensemble consistency analyses. Custom visualization tools highlight data quality warnings and potential sources of error, promoting vigilance against over-reliance and automation bias. Training materials and user manuals explicitly address cognitive biases inherent to AI-assisted decision-making, integrating best practices to sustain dispatcher critical judgment.

Operators can readily override or disregard model suggestions through dedicated control elements embedded in the interface, allowing immediate deactivation or reprioritization of incidents. A standardized ‘stop’ button safely suspends system operation, implemented with transactional rollback capabilities to maintain system integrity and avoid hanging processes during interruptions. Furthermore, dispatchers can trace the system’s processing pipeline and prior decision points via event logs, facilitating incident reviews and prompt anomaly diagnoses.

**Documentation of Special Category Data Processing for Bias Correction**

Priority Response Analytics processes selected special categories of personal data solely to detect and mitigate bias that may affect priority assessments. These data are processed in accordance with the strict necessity principle, with technical measures ensuring pseudonymization and access controls. Detailed records document the rationale for handling sensitive data, referencing the inability to achieve equivalent bias identification using non-sensitive attributes alone. System logs maintain transparent audit trails of bias correction activities, including algorithmic fairness evaluations performed during model retraining cycles on datasets of approximately 500,000 anonymized historical incidents. These compliance-centric data practices are integrated into data governance frameworks aligned with Regulations (EU) 2016/679 and (EU) 2018/1725, ensuring traceability and justifiability of sensitive data processing within the bias mitigation scope.
