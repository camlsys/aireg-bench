**Article 14**

**Design for Human Oversight and Interaction Modalities**

Contractual Separation Insight has been engineered with an emphasis on high-level summary presentation rather than detailed model explainability at runtime. The system relies on an ensemble approach combining random forest classifiers with large language models (LLMs) to analyze employee performance data and interpret contractual policies. However, it exposes only consolidated recommendation dashboards to human operators without surfacing granular decision trees from the random forest or token-level reasoning traces from the LLM ensemble. This architectural choice was made to strike a balance between providing actionable insights and managing cognitive load for end users, primarily HR and compliance professionals. User interfaces focus on aggregated indicators such as risk score bands, policy compliance flags, and predictive termination likelihoods. Interactive tooling for drilling down into intermediate model outputs or rationale is not included, thereby limiting dynamic interpretability during system operation.

**Oversight Objectives to Mitigate Risks**

Given the sensitivity of employment termination decisions, the human oversight framework centers on enabling responsible review of high-level recommendations to prevent misapplications that might infringe labor rights or fair process. The summarized dashboards highlight potential policy conflicts and flag cases with unusual model confidence intervals or atypical metric patterns, supporting anomaly detection by users. This risk-aware design anticipates reasonably foreseeable misuse scenarios, such as blind reliance on automated outputs or overlooking context-specific factors not encoded within the training data. By constraining output granularity and emphasizing aggregate results, the system directs attention to broad compliance validation rather than deep technical auditability. Deployment guidelines specify that operators must cross-reference system recommendations with human judgment and additional organizational information, reinforcing human-in-the-loop decision-making to minimize possible harms.

**Provisions for Oversight Measures and Responsibility Allocation**

The provider’s built-in oversight measures consist principally of interface design decisions and model performance validation conducted prior to market release. The ensemble models were trained on a proprietary corpus of over 120,000 anonymized HR records combined with 5,000 distinct labor policy documents, benchmarked internally with a mean F1-score of 0.87 for correct contract termination prediction. Testing included adversarial simulations to identify common failure modes, resulting in conservative thresholds for flagging uncertain recommendations within the dashboards. These pre-release mitigation efforts constitute the primary technical mechanism facilitating human oversight embedded by design. Conversely, measures recommended for deployment by the system user include procedural protocols for verifying recommendations and documenting overrides, which are communicated through accompanying operational manuals but remain outside the system software itself.

**Enabling User Understanding and Control within System Constraints**

While Contractual Separation Insight does not provide detailed model explananda during use, extensive training materials accompany the system to inform authorized operators of its methodological scope and limitations. These materials describe the nature of the random forest and LLM ensemble, the type and granularity of input data, and the rationale for presenting output as aggregated summaries. User awareness is further supported by dashboard elements explicitly advising against automation bias and encouraging critical appraisal of each recommendation. The dashboard design includes clearly labeled override controls, allowing users to reject or amend system suggestions. A manual “stop” function enables operators to suspend system operation safely in case of suspected malfunction or data anomalies. Operational logs capture user decisions and system outputs, facilitating traceability and post-hoc auditing consistent with data protection and accountability requirements. This approach enables operators to monitor system behavior sufficiently within the bounds imposed by the aggregated presentation format and the system’s intended use case.
