**Article 14**

### Design for Effective Human Oversight During Use

Contractual Separation Insight (CSI) is architected to facilitate continuous and effective human oversight throughout its operational lifecycle. The system integrates an ensemble of 150 decision trees within a random forest framework, generating quantitative risk scores based on structured employee performance metrics (e.g., attendance, productivity indices, disciplinary records totaling approximately 10,000 data points per case). These quantitative analyses are combined with outputs from domain-specific large language models (LLMs), fine-tuned on a corpus of over 50,000 anonymized labor policies and case precedents to interpret qualitative policy text relevant to employment termination.

Human-machine interface (HMI) tools are embedded natively, providing dynamic dashboards that visualize confidence intervals, key contributing features, and textual policy references associated with each recommendation. These interfaces present interpretable feature importance rankings and policy rationale excerpts, enabling users to track the logic behind every output. The system architecture supports real-time interaction with alerts for anomalous or outlier cases detected by continuous monitoring algorithms, allowing human reviewers to intervene promptly.

An active “stop” control mechanism is implemented as part of the HMI, enabling users to immediately suspend automated recommendation generation and request manual re-evaluation or audit trails. This design decision follows extensive usability testing with pilot end users, demonstrating a 95% success rate in enabling users to understand system recommendations within five minutes and effectively intervene when necessary.

### Risk Minimization through Human Oversight

Oversight capabilities are tailored to mitigate identified risks to fundamental rights, employee dignity, and procedural fairness that arise from automated contract termination decisions. CSI employs anomaly detection submodules trained on synthetic and real historical case scenarios to flag outputs with a potentially disproportionate impact on protected groups or inconsistent with applicable labor laws.

Human reviewers receive structured alerts highlighting potential risks, such as deviations from documented legal standards or empirical policy deviations uncovered through automated compliance checks. These alerts trigger mandatory secondary review workflows, ensuring that no termination recommendation proceeds without explicit human approval.

The oversight functionality is further supported by comprehensive logging of decision paths, enabling auditors to reconstruct and assess every recommendation retrospectively. This continuous oversight loop is designed to prevent risk persistence even under foreseeable misuse scenarios, such as accelerated high-volume processing requests or intentional data manipulation attempts.

### Proportionate Oversight Measures Aligned with Risk Profile

Given the high stakes in employment termination decisions and the system’s semi-autonomous mode of operation, CSI incorporates a two-pronged oversight approach:

(a) **Built-in measures:** Prior to deployment, the provider embedded transparency-enhancing features and anomaly flagging directly into the AI system’s core. For example, random forest confidence thresholds dynamically adjust the presentation of outputs to ensure borderline cases are flagged for manual review. The LLM components include fine-tuned calibration layers to detect overgeneralizations or contradictory policy interpretations. These safeguards are validated through iterative testing involving 5,000 simulated termination cases, ensuring alignment with regulatory and ethical benchmarks.

(b) **Deployer-implemented measures:** The provider supplies detailed guidance and standardized protocols enabling deployers to implement contextualized human oversight, including the designation of authorized human reviewers trained in interpreting both numerical risk scores and policy text outputs. Training materials emphasize recognition of automation bias and proper intervention methods, informed by provider-led workshops involving 100+ HR compliance professionals.

### Enabling Deployers for Adequate Human Oversight

To ensure deployers enable natural persons assigned to human oversight to properly monitor the system, CSI is delivered with a comprehensive suite of supporting materials and interface capabilities:

(a) **Understanding capacities and limitations:** The user interface clearly documents the system’s model scope, assumptions, and known limitations, such as constraints in interpreting emerging labor legislation not yet incorporated in the model’s knowledge base. Interactive educational modules embedded within the system present case studies illustrating potential edge cases and explain model decision boundaries.

(b) **Mitigating automation bias:** Explicit alerts and disclaimers are integrated within the user experience to maintain reviewer vigilance against automation bias. These prompts encourage active questioning of recommendations and clarify that outputs serve as advisory support rather than definitive decisions. User logs record instances of override or disregard to monitor adherence to oversight principles.

(c) **Interpretation tools:** Detailed interpretability features include SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)-based visualizations that break down trustworthiness of specific input features, enabling nuanced interpretation of outputs. Policy text snippets with source references are hyperlinked directly in the interface for quick cross-verification against original documents.

(d) **Decision authority:** The system explicitly empowers human decision-makers to override or disregard AI recommendations at any stage. Override actions are straightforwardly executed via the interface with immediate effect on downstream workflows, ensuring final decision authority remains with the natural person.

(e) **Intervention and safe halt procedures:** The HMI incorporates a “stop” button that halts ongoing batch processing or individual case evaluation, ensuring the system transitions to a safe idle state without jeopardizing data integrity. Protocols for emergency intervention are clearly documented and tested as part of standard operating procedures.

(f) **Documentation on data processing necessity:** Consistent with GDPR (Regulation (EU) 2016/679) and related data protection laws, CSI’s data processing activities, including use of special categories of personal data such as ethnicity or health-related absenteeism where explicitly authorized, are fully documented. Justifications are provided specifying that such data are strictly necessary to detect and mitigate biases that cannot be addressed through other, non-sensitive data. These records include detailed impact assessments and decisions validating the proportionality and necessity of processing special categories.

This holistic design approach ensures that human oversight is grounded in clear understanding, active engagement, and empowered decision-making, thereby aligning technical measures with the risk profile and use context of the Contractual Separation Insight system.
