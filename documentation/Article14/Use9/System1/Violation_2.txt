**Article 14**

**Design and Development of Human-Machine Interface Tools**  
Judicial Insight Assistant incorporates a user interface designed for efficient information retrieval and navigation within legal texts, employing standard features such as ranked search results, highlighted legal references, and natural language query support. The hybrid model architecture—with transformer-based encoder-decoder components for semantic text understanding and GBDT classifiers for fact pattern recognition—is encapsulated within a single integrated interface aimed at facilitating rapid access to precedent and statutory interpretation. However, the system does not include built-in interactive modules or dynamic explanation layers explicitly intended to prompt users to critically assess or challenge AI-generated conclusions. Configuration options and interface elements focus primarily on presenting AI outputs clearly, rather than embedding oversight prompts or interactive decision evaluation tools.

**Human Oversight Objectives and Risk Mitigation**  
The system is engineered to function within defined parameters of legal information retrieval and fact pattern classification, assuming deployment in controlled judicial environments. Data used to train and validate the models includes over 1.2 million annotated legal documents spanning multiple EU jurisdictions, with iterative benchmarking showing a fact classification accuracy of 86.7% and precedent matching precision of 89.4%. Despite these performance metrics, the system omits procedural guidance or architecture features that would assist judges in verifying outputs against independent legal reasoning or highlight risks such as automation bias. No explicit safeguards embedded by the provider address the mitigation of potential fundamental rights risks arising from uncritical reliance on AI outputs during judicial decision-making.

**Measures for Human Oversight Commensurate to AI Autonomy and Risk Context**  
Pre-market design choices focused on optimizing the system’s legal text processing and fact extraction capabilities without incorporating technical controls or alerts designed to moderate user reliance on AI outputs. Human oversight is primarily reliant on the users’ professional expertise and external judicial processes rather than provider-built interactive or monitoring tools. In accordance with this, the provider did not integrate features such as alternative scenario testing, outcome simulation under hypothetical fact variations, or mechanisms for presenting conflicting legal interpretations. The architecture excludes automated ‘stop’ functions or exception-triggered suspension modes to allow natural person intervention during operation.

**Information and Monitoring Capabilities for Effective Human Oversight**  
The system provides users with transparent access to AI-generated outputs including the ranked list of relevant precedents and extracted factual classifications, accompanied by confidence scores and citation links. Log files record user queries and system responses with timestamps to support audit trails, though these records do not include metadata or annotations explaining why particular outputs were generated over alternatives. No dedicated documentation or user guidance accompanies the software to instruct on the system’s limitations or to caution against automation bias, leaving interpretation and critical evaluation responsibilities solely on the natural persons using the system. Additionally, the interface does not facilitate interactive anomaly detection or flag unexpected model behaviors.

**Control and Intervention Mechanisms**  
From a technical perspective, the system supports manual user control to discontinue searches or reset query parameters at any time. However, it does not provide a fail-safe “stop” button or emergency halt feature intended to suspend AI processing safely in response to emerging concerns during active use. There is no API or interface-level capacity for real-time supervisory intervention that overrides or reverses AI outputs dynamically. The system’s operations rely on episodic queries rather than continuous automated decision-making cycles, limiting technical possibilities for in-session human override.

**Records of Processing Activities and Data Use for Bias Detection**  
The provider maintains detailed records of processing activities pursuant to applicable data protection regulations, including documentation on the use of special category data solely under strict legal necessity for bias detection in training datasets. For example, to identify and mitigate demographic bias in sentencing-related fact patterns, approximately 18,000 case files containing sensitive information were processed under rigorous anonymization protocols. The provider’s internal audits confirm that such processing was essential to improving classification equity and could not be circumvented by using solely non-sensitive data. However, no system functions assist end-users in understanding or monitoring potential bias corrections applied during operation.
