**Article 14**

**Human Oversight Design and Interaction Capabilities**  
The Political Influence Analyzer employs state-of-the-art transformer-based encoder-decoder architectures with a focus on generating tailored political messaging at scale. From the provider’s perspective, the system’s design prioritizes automated content generation without integrating any embedded mechanisms for real-time human interaction or intervention during message generation. No dedicated user interface components are implemented to facilitate continuous human monitoring or supervision of output creation, such as dashboards displaying confidence levels, detected content risks, or anomaly alerts. The system’s API endpoints deliver generated text directly to deployer-operated platforms, with no built-in controls to pause or halt generation mid-process. This design choice reflects a streamlined pipeline architecture intended for high-throughput communication campaigns, where human oversight is not embedded within the system’s operational loop.

**Oversight Objectives and Risk Considerations**  
Despite the system’s capability to generate highly persuasive and individualized political messaging, no internal safeguards exist to prevent or minimize potential risks related to health, safety, or fundamental rights arising from its use. Risks associated with misleading or divisive content, manipulation of vulnerable or marginalized voter groups, and amplification of biased political narratives are acknowledged as foreseeable misuse scenarios inherent in the intended operational setting. However, the provider’s development strategy does not incorporate risk mitigation features addressing these specific harms. Instead, the system's resilience and correctness are evaluated solely on metrics related to language fluency, thematic coherence, and relevance to input profiles, without domain-specific content safety filters, bias detection modules, or output flagging capabilities.

**Risk-Proportionate Measures Embedded by the Provider**  
In accordance with the provider’s assessment of technological feasibility and intended deployment context, no proactive, built-in risk management or human oversight tools are included in the AI system prior to placing it on the market. There are no technical interventions implemented such as on-the-fly content filtering for divisiveness, explicit detection or annotation of manipulative language, or user alerts signaling output that could negatively impact vulnerable groups. Model training datasets—comprising approximately 300 million public political communication documents and social media content—were curated for language diversity and representation of multiple viewpoints but were not filtered or annotated for specific bias or manipulation risk reduction. Model evaluation focused on standard benchmarks (e.g., BLEU > 0.68, ROUGE-L > 0.72) pertaining to text quality rather than content ethics or compliance with fundamental rights protections.

**Oversight Responsibilities Assigned to the Deployer**  
The provider delivers the Political Influence Analyzer as a flexible API endpoint accompanied by detailed technical documentation on model architecture, input format specifications, and performance characteristics. The system’s deployment package includes no built-in user interface features designed to support the oversight role of natural persons, such as stop controls, override functions, or output explanation tools. The provider explicitly delegates responsibility for implementing oversight measures appropriate to the deployment environment to the end-user deployers. This includes any necessary human-in-the-loop checkpoints, content moderation workflows, or monitoring infrastructure to detect misuse or harmful outputs. The provider’s documentation specifies that the system is a general-purpose political messaging generator without embedded mechanisms for alerting human overseers when outputs may manipulate vulnerable voters or propagate misleading political content.

**Enablement of Human Oversight in Operation**  
Natural persons assigned oversight at the deployer level receive technical documentation outlining the system’s input requirements, output characteristics, and typical failure modes, such as potential hallucinations or repetition. However, given the absence of integrated interpretability or anomaly detection tools within the system, humans must rely on external processes to understand the AI’s capacities and limitations. There are no interface elements that mitigate automation bias or help end-users interpret the generated content’s reliability in context. The provider does not supply functionality allowing natural persons to interrupt or halt output generation directly; consequently, deployers would need to implement separate control mechanisms outside the system to enable stopping operations safely. Additionally, the system’s data processing records comply with applicable data protection regulations, documenting the limited use of personal data solely for model training purposes but do not include reasoning related to bias correction or safeguards concerning sensitive information, as no such data processing is performed for these objectives.
