**Article 9**

**Establishment and Scope of the Risk Management System**  
Meridian Labor Solutions has instituted a risk management system specific to the Legal Termination Assessment Framework (LTAF), consistent with the iterative lifecycle approach mandated for high-risk AI systems. This system systematically identifies and analyses risks related to the use of LTAF for contract termination assessments, with a focus on health, safety, and fundamental rights. Given the system’s operational context in human resources, particular attention is paid to risks impacting individual rights, including the potential for disproportionate adverse impacts on minority and vulnerable groups. The identification phase incorporated comprehensive domain research and stakeholder input, highlighting nuances in legal contract interpretation and the inherent ambiguity of termination criteria. Known risks include uneven contract termination outcomes arising from subtle biases embedded in training data, which notably affect minority groups. Reasonably foreseeable misuse scenarios, such as deployment without sufficient human oversight or misinterpretation of ambiguous legal contexts by system users, were also analyzed. This phase was informed by a dataset of approximately 1.5 million anonymized personnel and contract records collected across multiple EU jurisdictions.

**Risk Estimation and Evaluation Process**  
Risk estimation within the LTAF lifecycle has been based on quantitative and qualitative evaluation frameworks. The gradient-boosted decision trees (GBDT) and transformer-based NLP models were assessed on performance metrics disaggregated by demographic subgroups, revealing variation in false positive and false negative termination recommendations at the 7-12% range between majority and minority populations. These discrepancies reflect subtle biases present in the training corpora, which include legal documents and employee records with limited minority representation. Sensitivity analyses identified that contextual ambiguity in legal texts, especially concerning contract clause interpretation, exacerbates inconsistencies in outputs. The evaluation extended to stress testing under misapplication scenarios, such as incomplete records and atypical contract conditions. Testing prior to deployment involved bench-marking across three EU labor law variants to capture cross-jurisdictional legal nuances, resulting in documented residual risks related to minority group assessments. While identified, these residual risks were not quantitatively integrated into a formal residual risk acceptance framework.

**Incorporation of Post-Market Monitoring and Data Feedback Loops**  
As part of ongoing evaluation, post-market monitoring mechanisms have been instituted, capturing anonymized system decisions and user feedback to detect emergent patterns of disproportionate adverse outcomes. Data collection captures metadata enabling stratified analyses, including ethnicity and protected characteristics where legally permissible. However, current feedback aggregation processes have not included systematic, quantitative estimation of residual risks related to minority groups’ termination assessments. The monitoring infrastructure supports notifications and trend identification but does not yet enforce dynamic risk re-evaluation thresholds or trigger automated risk mitigation measures for bias drift. Periodic reviews are scheduled biannually to update risk profiles and adjust model parameters as necessary, but the linkage between monitoring results and risk acceptance criteria remains under development.

**Technical and Organizational Risk Mitigation Measures**  
During design and development, Meridian Labor Solutions incorporated several mitigation layers addressing identified risks from dataset imbalance and legal ambiguity. These include data augmentation to improve minority subgroup representation, adversarial testing with legal experts simulating edge-case contract scenarios, and ensemble decision heuristics combining outputs from GBDT and transformer NLP modules to moderate model bias and enhance robustness. Explainability modules provide deployers with feature importance attributions and legal rationale summaries to support transparent human review. Despite these measures, the system’s capacity to fully eliminate risks stemming from nuanced legal ambiguities and latent data biases remains limited. Consequently, risk controls address primary discrimination vectors but acknowledge residual inconsistent outcomes for minority groups.

Organizationally, Meridian Labor Solutions mandates training materials and usage guidelines intended for deployers to clarify the system’s scope, limitations, and the critical importance of human oversight in ambiguous cases. Deployment guidance emphasizes the need for context-aware legal interpretation and cautions against sole reliance on automated outputs for final termination decisions. However, no formal requirement or mechanism is enforced within the framework to quantify or formally accept residual risks associated with minority group inconsistencies, nor to adjust system parameters dynamically in reaction to monitored risk trends.

**Risk Management Measures Integration and Effectiveness Considerations**  
The risk management design accounted for the interaction of technical and operational measures, balancing system performance optimization with the deployment context. Testing included multiple phases over the development lifecycle, applying established benchmarks such as F1-score parity thresholds and subgroup calibration error metrics, achieving an overall accuracy of 87% but with a demographic parity gap of approximately 10%. Real-world pilot deployments under controlled conditions involved human-in-the-loop protocols, demonstrating that combined system-human decision processes reduce adverse impacts compared to standalone automated recommendations; however, pilot evaluations noted residual inconsistencies in minority termination outcomes relative to majority counterparts.

These findings inform the risk acceptance posture, wherein Meridian Labor Solutions currently classifies residual risks arising from ambiguous contract interpretation and biased training data as areas for further mitigation but has not yet established quantified acceptance thresholds or declared such residual risks acceptable in formal risk reports. This gap reflects known challenges in fully addressing legal ambiguity and nuanced bias effects with existing model architectures and training data, which remain focal points for ongoing research and system refinement.

**Consideration of Vulnerable Groups Including Minors**  
While the system chiefly addresses adult employee records, the risk management process considered potential adverse effects on vulnerable populations, including persons under 18 and other protected groups. The training datasets predominantly exclude minor employees due to jurisdictional labor protections, yet the framework’s known limitations in handling minority group data are explicitly analyzed. Extension of risk controls to systematically estimate and mitigate differential impacts on vulnerable groups beyond minorities has not been fully realized, reflecting a developmental focus on primary adult worker categories. Deployment documentation states that users must assess appropriateness before applying the system in contexts involving minors or other vulnerable persons, without embedding automatic safeguards or exclusion criteria at the system level.

**Testing Regime and Compliance Verification**  
LTAF testing adhered to iterative validation cycles throughout development, largely performed in simulated and sandbox environments that reflect legal contract variation and workforce demographics typical in the EU context. These tests verify compliance with functional specifications and basic fairness metrics under defined probabilistic thresholds. Validation includes domain expert review of ambiguous cases to ascertain system interpretive limitations, supporting continuous improvement insights. Although prior to market release extensive testing confirmed system stability and consistency for intended use, coverage gaps remain in comprehensive risk quantification for residual minority-related biases and legal ambiguity effects. Post-release, testing protocols mandate updates and recalibrations aligned with evolving labor laws and user feedback but do not yet mandate systematic re-assessment of residual risk acceptance or explicit bias minimization refinements.
