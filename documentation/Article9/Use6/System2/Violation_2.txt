**Article 9**  

**Establishment and Scope of the Risk Management System**  
A dedicated risk management system was established to address risks arising from Contractual Separation Insight throughout its entire lifecycle, from data acquisition and model training phases through deployment and maintenance. The system’s primary focus lies on ensuring high data accuracy and maintaining robust model performance for consistent outcome reliability in contractual termination decision support. While identified risks center on prediction errors, data quality lapses, and model degradation over time, foreseeable risks related to biased or discriminatory outcomes affecting vulnerable employee groups were not explicitly incorporated into the formal risk identification process. This scope decision was influenced by the system design emphasis on technical correctness and adherence to documented HR metrics and policy language, rather than systematic bias evaluation.

**Iterative Risk Analysis Procedures**  
Risk analysis is conducted continuously and iteratively, with scheduled quarterly reviews aligned with Agile development cycles. These reviews incorporate statistical assessment of input data integrity, performance benchmarks, and drift detection across the ensemble random forest and LLM components. The identification and analysis phases primarily assess risks impacting model accuracy—such as class imbalance effects, noisy labeling in historical HR datasets, and out-of-distribution sample detection—to ensure precision and recall metrics meet predefined thresholds (e.g., average F1-score maintained above 0.87 on a validation dataset of 150,000 anonymized employee records). Risk estimation also considers scenarios of misuse, such as overreliance on model recommendation scores without human review, but vulnerability-specific bias risks are not proactively analyzed. Post-market data are collected via deployer feedback logs and system usage telemetry, supporting retrospective performance validation but are not systematically reviewed for potential disparate impact on protected employee characteristics.

**Risk Evaluation and Residual Risk Assessment**  
Risk evaluation metrics focus heavily on quantitative indicators of model efficacy, including false positive and false negative rates in predicting contract termination cases. Residual risk is considered acceptable based on the system’s observed overall accuracy and compliance with HR policy constraints embedded in the LLM interpretative modules. Mitigation measures primarily target technical faults and reliability—for example, model retraining triggered by detection of concept drift exceeding a 5% threshold in predictive accuracy or data shifts beyond a 10% variance from the training distribution. Technical strategies include ensemble averaging for variance reduction and explicit policy rule-checking layers to prevent inconsistent outputs. No formal evaluation of residual risks related to systemic bias or disparate impact against minority or vulnerable employee groups, such as pregnant workers, is documented or integrated into residual risk judgments.

**Risk Management Measures and Technical Controls**  
The implemented risk controls reflect current industry standards for model robustness in 2025. These encompass: advanced feature engineering pipelines normalizing quantitative performance metrics; balanced sampling techniques to address skew in the training dataset; and interpretability mechanisms leveraging LLM attention weights aligned with HR policy lexicons to increase transparency. Model validation employs cross-validation with stratification to reduce sampling bias in training and testing splits. Additionally, a simulation environment emulates contract termination decision scenarios to stress-test model outputs under varied input conditions. While these measures reduce errors resulting from data quality and algorithmic variability, no targeted technical interventions—such as fairness-aware learning, bias audits, or subgroup performance evaluations—have been integrated to explicitly detect or mitigate discriminatory outcomes relating to vulnerable employee demographics.

**Testing and Verification Protocols**  
Contractual Separation Insight undergoes rigorous testing prior to deployment and throughout its operational lifespan. Testing includes offline validation against extensive internal benchmarks and real-world condition simulations using anonymized HR records sourced from multiple corporate clients to capture diverse employment contexts. Model performance is quantitatively assessed via metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and area under the ROC curve, with acceptance criteria calibrated on historical system use cases involving approximately 200,000 total employee records. Testing scripts are version-controlled and automated within a CI/CD pipeline, allowing for continuous verification following each model update. Despite comprehensive performance testing, verification protocols do not include assessments of how recommendations may disproportionately affect specific vulnerable subgroups defined by protected characteristics or physiological states. Furthermore, adverse impact analyses recommended for vulnerable groups under Article 9(9) are not formally documented as part of the testing regime.

**Provision of Information and Operational Guidance**  
Technical documentation accompanying the system provides detailed information on algorithmic components, data processing methods, and limitations related to data accuracy and model confidence intervals. User guidance materials emphasize the need for human-in-the-loop review, especially when recommendations influence final contract termination decisions. Training modules intended for deployers focus on interpreting probabilistic scores, understanding system alerts on data anomalies, and following compliance with applicable labor laws as encoded in the policy analysis layers. These educational resources are designed assuming deployers possess foundational HR expertise and technical literacy appropriate for moderate AI system interaction. However, explicit guidance addressing awareness or mitigation of potential biases affecting vulnerable employee groups is absent from these materials.  

**Consideration of Vulnerable Groups**  
In alignment with the system’s current risk management framework, the assessment of risks related to adverse impacts on vulnerable groups—such as employees under age 18, pregnant employees, or minority populations—is limited. Although data sets used include demographic markers, no systematic analysis or risk evaluation targeting the differential performance or outcome disparities for these groups has been performed. The risk management measures do not incorporate specialized procedures for detecting or mitigating systemic bias or ensuring equitable treatment across all employee segments as part of model design or deployment safeguards.
