**Article 9**

### Establishment and Scope of the Risk Management System  
Lexicon Analytics Corporation has established a formal risk management system specifically tailored for the Legal Context Navigator (LCN), operational throughout the AI system lifecycle from initial development to deployment and maintenance. This continuous, iterative process involves structured documentation revisions and scheduled assessments aligned with the system’s intended use by judicial authorities. The system’s technical architecture is based on an encoder-only transformer large language model, trained on an aggregated corpus exceeding 500 million textual data points, including statutory law, regulations, and court opinions from multiple EU jurisdictions. The corpus excludes datasets likely to contain direct personal identifiers, thus focusing the risk assessment primarily on technical and operational hazards, such as system downtime, input errors, or processing delays, as evidenced by internal failure mode analyses conducted during pre-deployment phases.

### Identification and Evaluation of Risks  
Risk analysis for LCN emphasizes predictable risks related to system stability and accuracy in retrieving and processing legal texts under intended operating conditions. The system’s technical monitoring logs record unexpected downtime events at an average rate of less than 0.5 per 1,000 operational hours, with automated error handling remediating 95% of recognized input errors, such as malformed queries or ambiguous fact pattern entries. The risk management protocols identify degradation scenarios under unusually high simultaneous query loads, with fallback mechanisms including partial service scaling and user alerts. However, semantic risks potentially arising from biases or skewed legal interpretations embedded in training data are not systematically analyzed or quantified within the risk framework. No risk quantification models or bias impact assessments were included to evaluate disproportionate effects on minority or vulnerable judicial subjects, reflecting a focus constrained to technical and operational risk categories.

### Risk Management Measures Adopted  
Technical mitigation measures concentrate on ensuring system availability, verifiable document indexing, and query output consistency. These include continuous integration and deployment pipelines, automated regression testing against a benchmark suite containing 20,000 annotated legal references, and routine validation of response time thresholds (target 95th percentile < 300 ms under standard load). User-facing documentation includes clear error reporting protocols and operational guidelines for judicial assistants to verify outputs. While these measures effectively reduce residual risks associated with downtime and input processing errors, no targeted mechanisms to mitigate risks related to semantic bias or adverse impacts on fundamental rights have been integrated. The system’s design and training procedures incorporate routine removal of corrupted text sources but do not extend to the identification or active correction of latent discriminatory patterns in legal interpretations.

### Testing and Verification Procedures  
LCN undergoes multi-phase testing aligned with the development lifecycle, including unit testing of model components, integration testing with legal databases, and simulated user scenario testing involving factual-law matching tasks. Metrics tracked include precision and recall of legal citations, targeting over 92% accuracy across diverse case types. Testing scenarios also involve stress tests simulating concurrent judicial user activity, with performance measured against probabilistic thresholds for uptime and query success rates. Real-world evaluation using anonymized query logs from pilot judicial authorities is performed bi-annually. Nonetheless, these evaluations focus exclusively on technical performance and operational robustness without systematic exploration of potential semantic biases or discriminatory outputs that might affect protected groups, nor are there documented protocols for user feedback relating to fairness or interpretative equity.

### Consideration of Vulnerable Groups and Fundamental Rights  
Although the system design documentation acknowledges the presence of vulnerable populations in the judicial process, risk assessments fail to incorporate specific analysis addressing their protection. No differential impact testing against data subsets representing cases involving minors, ethnic minorities, or other vulnerable categories has been performed. No risk scenarios consider adverse outcomes arising from entrenched biases in training data or model inference that could influence judicial decision-making processes negatively for these groups. Provider decisions have thus been limited to technical risk mitigation, without extending to semantic fairness evaluations or fundamental rights impact assessments. The absence of these analyses corresponds to a delineation of the system’s scope, which centers on operational risk control rather than on societal or ethical risk management.

### Integration with External Risk Management Requirements  
The risk management framework for LCN is designed to be standalone and compliant with the specified provisions of Article 9 of the EU AI Act. While Lexicon Analytics Corporation recognizes the potential for overlapping regulatory obligations under Union law relating to legal technology products, no integration with broader internal risk management processes concerning fundamental rights or bias mitigation has been implemented. There is no evidence of coordinated compliance efforts that embed semantic risk considerations or rights-based safeguards within the provider’s documented procedures. The risk management lifecycle remains primarily focused on conventional reliability, accuracy, and security dimensions inherent to AI-driven legal text processing systems.
