**Article 12**

**Technical Logging Architecture and Event Capture Scope**

The Credit Evaluation Network employs a logging framework configured to record discrete snapshots of credit decisions and corresponding applicant data exclusively during scheduled maintenance windows, which occur on a biweekly basis. These capture points include fully serialized instances of input feature vectors representing applicant financial and demographic attributes, the decision output (credit score and classification label), and relevant metadata such as processing timestamps, model version identifiers, and system state indicators at the time of evaluation. Logging is implemented through a dedicated data pipeline that extracts this information atomically at the conclusion of each evaluation cycle designated by maintenance scheduling. Between maintenance intervals, continuous logging of transaction-level events, parameter updates, or intermediate algorithmic recalibrations is explicitly not performed. This approach consolidates snapshots necessary for periodic auditability while limiting storage volume and exposure to potential data leakage risks.

The rationale for this snapshot-based, periodic logging strategy stems from compliance with operational protocols intended to balance comprehensive record-keeping with privacy and data minimization principles. By avoiding continuous capture of all intermediary events, the system ensures retention of high-value information pertinent to credit decision outcomes without incurring overhead or complications from excessive accumulation of transient model dynamics or parameter tuning logs that occur during internal model development cycles.

**Traceability Measures Supporting Risk Identification and Post-Market Monitoring**

The logging design facilitates traceability at levels pertinent to identifying potential risks as specified under Article 79(1) by consistently recording applicant profiles and corresponding credit decisions that constitute the functional output of the AI system. These records include both approved and declined credit inquiries, enabling retrospective detection of decision anomalies or bias patterns through correlation with applicant demographics and financial features. The system’s version control integration tags each snapshot with immutable model version hashes and baseline configuration parameters, ensuring traceability to specific algorithm states. This aids in recognizing substantial system modifications, for example, transitions between model recalibration cycles that are reflected in new version markers inserted into logs generated at subsequent maintenance events.

For post-market monitoring aligned with Article 72, the stored data snapshots form a stable basis for statistical assessments of model stability and performance drift over time. Aggregated log analyses performed at regular intervals verify that credit scoring distributions and false positive/negative rates remain within nominal operational ranges established during initial validation. Consequently, operational teams can detect and investigate deviations potentially indicative of deteriorated model behavior or external risk factors manifesting in input data shifts.

**Monitoring Operational Integrity and Scheduled Log Activation**

In adherence to monitoring requirements referenced in Article 26(5), the system’s logging framework is explicitly designed for activation solely through scheduled maintenance triggers rather than unsupervised, continuous event capture. This controlled activation prevents inadvertent or ad hoc event logging which could generate unmanageable data volumes or capture sensitive model internals occurring at runtime. The logging software interfaces strictly with credit decision endpoints, recording finalized outcomes rather than intermediate computational stages. Deployment configurations enforce role-based access and encryption controls over log storage to maintain data confidentiality and audit trail integrity.

Overall, these logging practices collectively deliver a traceability model adequate to the intended purpose of the Credit Evaluation Network, confining captured event data to substantive decision points aligned with scheduled operational reviews while precluding extraneous recording of model updates, parameter tuning, or recalibrations occurring between maintenance activities.
