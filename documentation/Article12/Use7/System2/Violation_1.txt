**Article 12**

### Technical Implementation of Event Logging

The Consumer Credit Transformer integrates a logging framework designed to record system events automatically once credit decisions are finalized and communicated. The logging system captures discrete credit decision outputs, including final credit approval or rejection status, associated confidence scores generated by the final inference layer, timestamps, and unique application identifiers. This enables an auditable trail of decision outcomes directly linked to individual credit applications. Internal model inference stages—such as intermediate transformer layer activations, self-attention maps, or embedded feature transformations—and all data preprocessing steps, including normalization, categorical encoding, and missing value imputations, execute automatically within isolated runtime containers but are not logged. This design choice aligns with operational requirements for efficiency and data minimization by limiting log volume and exposure of sensitive intermediate representations, balancing traceability with privacy considerations.

### Rationale for Logging Scope and Depth

Focusing logging exclusively on finalized credit decisions and associated metadata reflects a deliberate provider decision grounded in current best practices for privacy preservation and operational transparency. Since intermediate model inferences and preprocessing occur as internal transient computations without direct user- or regulator-observable impact, excluding them from logs reduces unnecessary data proliferation without compromising the capacity to reconstruct decision outcomes. Furthermore, this approach maintains the performance efficiency of the system in high-throughput deployment environments handling over 500,000 monthly credit applications. The post-hoc review processes at Veritas Risk Solutions rely primarily on recorded decision outcomes combined with application metadata, which are sufficient for tracing potential risks or unexplained decision patterns as required under high-risk AI governance frameworks.

### Traceability Relevant to Risk Identification and Monitoring

The logging captures all necessary data elements that facilitate the identification of potentially risky decision patterns and substantial system modifications. Recorded logs include version identifiers of the operative model weights and feature schema to enable traceability of the system state at the time of each decision, supporting impact analysis in the event of model updates or drift. By maintaining a consistent log of credit decision outcomes, including concrete indicators such as rejection reasons from the final scoring thresholds and aggregated confidence levels, the system enables downstream monitoring tools to perform anomaly detection on decision distributions and to flag potential systemic biases or degradation, consistent with the obligations under Article 79(1). Post-market monitoring workflows leverage these logs to assess system stability, while operational monitoring tools track decision volumes and confidence metrics in near real-time to detect deviations from expected behavioral baselines.

### Conformance to System Lifecycle Logging Requirements

While preprocessing and intermediate inferential activities are fundamental to the model’s function, these processes execute within secure, ephemeral modules that do not persist logs but adhere to rigorous internal audit protocols outside the live logging system. Veritas Risk Solutions documents these internal mechanisms extensively in separate technical and security assessments but excludes them from automatic event logging to comply with data minimization principles and reduce information overload for compliance audits. The automated data pipeline feeding preprocessing and inference modules is subject to strict input validation and schema conformity checks upstream, ensuring that only valid and complete data enters the scoring phase logged under the Consumer Credit Transformer. Consequently, the scope of the recorded logs provides a targeted yet comprehensive record sufficient for retrospective analysis, system validation, and regulatory oversight aligned with the intended use of the system in consumer credit decisioning.
