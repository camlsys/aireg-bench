**Article 12**  

**Logging Architecture and Scope**  
Judicial Insight Assistant incorporates a structured logging framework designed to capture and preserve critical interactions and outputs throughout the operational lifecycle of the system. The logging subsystem records each user query submitted by the judicial authority or legal researcher, as well as the corresponding final outputs generated by the hybrid AI model. These outputs include the synthesized legal research findings, relevant precedent summaries, fact pattern classifications, and interpretative suggestions delivered to end users. By design, the logging architecture excludes the capture of intermediate parameter data or internal state transitions of the gradient boosted decision tree (GBDT) component, particularly during iterative updates triggered by cases classified as “ambiguous.” This delineation ensures that while traceability of user interaction and final system decisions is maintained, sensitive parameter adjustments that do not directly affect the recorded output remain unlogged.  

**Rationale for Logging Decisions**  
The decision to restrict logs to user queries and final outputs arises from a considered balance between traceability requirements and operational confidentiality. Intermediate parameter updates within the GBDT classifier, specifically weight coefficient adjustments responding to “ambiguous” cases, involve continuous adaptive learning mechanisms designed to refine classification accuracy without altering externally observable behaviors in an immediately discernible manner. Logging these internal state changes could generate extensive sensitive data volumes potentially duplicative of final outcomes, complicating data governance and increasing compliance risks without commensurate benefits for post-market oversight. Moreover, the system’s final outputs fully encapsulate the legally relevant decision pathways as consumed by end users, thus aligning the scope of logged events with the traceability objectives specified for high-risk AI systems.  

**Events Recorded to Support Risk Identification and Post-market Monitoring**  
Audit logs systematically include timestamped records of all user queries and respective outputs, thereby enabling reconstruction of decision contexts in cases of dispute or review. This granularity supports identification of scenarios wherein the system’s interpretations may have contributed to unexpected judicial outcomes or exhibited deviations from intended behavior, covering the risk categories outlined in Article 79(1). The logging mechanism also facilitates the aggregation of operational data applicable to post-market monitoring activities, enabling trend analysis, error incidence tracking, and verification of consistency across successive software iterations. By focusing on final outputs rather than raw parameter shifts, the system maintains a stable audit trail robust enough to evaluate functional integrity and user-facing performance over time without exposing underlying model update details that do not directly influence immediate output integrity.  

**Operation Monitoring and Compliance with Article 26(5) Requirements**  
To maintain continuous operational oversight as required by Article 26(5), the logging framework integrates with the system’s monitoring dashboards and alerting modules, which correlate logged outputs and user queries with real-time system availability and response latency metrics. This integration ensures that any anomalies detected at the interface between user input and AI-generated conclusions—including those potentially arising from non-logged GBDT internal updates—are flagged for review. The system architecture separates internal machine learning adaptations from the audit log pipeline, minimizing performance overhead and data storage during high-frequency parameter updates, while preserving an effective means to trace operational behaviors directly relevant to judicial users. This approach supports a scalable, secure logging posture aligned with current standards for high-risk AI systems deployed in judicial contexts.
