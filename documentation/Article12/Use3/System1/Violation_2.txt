**Article 12**

### Event Logging Framework and Scope

The Adaptive Learning Outcome Analyzer incorporates an automated logging subsystem designed to document the system’s essential outputs throughout its operational lifetime. This subsystem actively captures final assessment scores—expressed as aggregated quantitative metrics synthesizing individual student performance across multiple dimensions—and summary feedback outputs, which consist of concise natural language explanations and learning path recommendations generated by the transformer-based models. These outputs represent the system’s conclusive determinations intended for consumption by educators and learners.

The logging design deliberately excludes the recording of intermediate model states, such as confidence scores at various processing stages, dynamic threshold adjustments within decision logic, or detected deviations in input data distributions (e.g., shifts in student response patterns relative to training data). This limitation aligns with a design rationale prioritizing operational efficiency, data privacy considerations, and the reduction of log volume and sensitivity. Logs are stored securely using encrypted, tamper-evident mechanisms ensuring integrity and confidentiality, with access restricted to authorized compliance and auditing personnel.

### Rationale for Event Selection and Implications for Traceability

The selection of logged events focuses on the capture of final actionable outcomes to support system traceability relative to the educational context and end-user requirements. Final assessment scores serve as objective, verifiable indicators of learner performance, facilitating retrospective evaluation and regulatory audits. Summary feedback outputs consolidate system reasoning into interpretable narratives, enabling educators to understand learning gaps and justify pedagogical adjustments.

While intermediate states such as model confidence levels and threshold tuning could provide finer-grained insights into operational nuances and potential risks, these are intentionally omitted from persistent logs. Consequently, direct traceability of latent model uncertainty and real-time threshold adaptations is not supported, potentially limiting root-cause analysis in instances of unexpected system behavior or risk manifestations. Similarly, the absence of recorded alerts related to input data distribution shifts means that systemic changes in learner population characteristics may elude detection via logged records.

### Support for Post-Market Monitoring and Operation Oversight

The logging approach facilitates post-market monitoring mandated under the relevant regulatory framework by enabling correlation of logged final outputs with external performance indicators collected by deployers (e.g., educator feedback, academic progression statistics). This supports high-level assessments of system effectiveness and safety over time. By providing a consistent record of assessment results and feedback summaries, the logs underpin longitudinal studies of educational outcomes shaped by the AI’s recommendations.

In operational terms, monitoring the system’s correct function is enabled predominantly through the availability of final scores and feedback logs, supplemented by system health metrics and runtime performance indicators that are logged separately by infrastructure-level monitoring tools. However, the absence of intermediate inference data in logs restricts the depth of operational diagnostics available solely from the Analyzer’s event records, necessitating complementary telemetry approaches at deployment sites for comprehensive oversight per Article 26(5).

### Technical Architecture of Logging Implementation

The system’s logging module is integrated with the transformer-based encoder-decoder inference pipeline at its terminus, capturing output tensors post-final classification and natural language generation stages. Logs are generated in JSON format with time-stamped entries identifying student and session context (pseudonymized to protect privacy), final numeric scores across competencies, and textual summary feedback fields. Log entries are aggregated in real time and transmitted to a centralized, secure logging service employing role-based access controls and automated log retention policies compliant with data governance standards.

No intermediate layer outputs, such as token-level confidence distributions or intermediate thresholds used in adaptive scoring, are serialized or stored. Detection logic for input data distribution shifts operates during model inference via internal statistical comparators but triggers no persistent logging event; associated alerts are confined to ephemeral internal metrics to balance confidentiality and system responsiveness without expanding recorded data volume.

### Compliance Design Considerations and Industry Context

Aligning with prevailing 2025 AI audit standards in educational technology, Meridian Cognition Technologies prioritized final output logging as the minimal yet effective approach to satisfy Article 12 requirements while mitigating risks related to sensitive auxiliary data exposure. This decision reflects a common industry trade-off balancing traceability with operational scalability and privacy, particularly in systems processing large volumes of personal educational data.

The logging architecture supports rigorous post-market evaluation through stable, reproducible outcome records while relying on deployment-level monitoring solutions for comprehensive system health and anomaly detection. This compartmentalization is consistent with standard practices in transformer-based assessment systems and accords with typical risk management frameworks applied to adaptive educational AI tools.
