**Article 12**

**Logging Architecture and Event Recording**

Contractual Separation Insight incorporates a comprehensive logging infrastructure designed to enable automatic recording of operational events throughout the system’s lifetime. This infrastructure captures discrete instances of final decision outputs and associated rationale generated by the ensemble of random forest classifiers and large language model components. Each logged entry includes the timestamp, the anonymized job or case identifier, the final contract termination recommendation, and the key policy references cited in the decision-making process. To support traceability while respecting the compliance profile constraints, logging explicitly excludes intermediate metadata related to ongoing model retraining workflows and runtime input feature validation flags. This design choice prevents the recording of transient retraining states or detected input inconsistencies that might forecast risk escalations, thereby minimizing the surface of logged data to stable, outcome-oriented events only.

**Traceability of Potential Risk and Substantial Changes**

In alignment with Article 12(2)(a), the system’s logs are structured to reflect events directly relevant to identifying situations that might present immediate operational risks or signify substantial changes in system performance. While the system continuously executes integrity checks and monitors input data quality internally, these process-level alerts are not logged. Instead, the logs focus on the final outcomes of model inference and decision synthesis, allowing assessors to identify unusual decision patterns or shifts by comparing periodic output distributions. Substantial modifications to the system, such as deployment of a new model ensemble iteration or LLM update, are documented through distinct, versioned deployment metadata stored in configuration management databases outside the log files, preserving separation between operational logs and developmental change records.

**Support for Post-Market Monitoring**

To facilitate post-market monitoring as required by Article 12(2)(b), the logging framework captures each conclusion delivered to end users, including the final contract termination advice and the associated confidence scores derived from ensemble aggregation. The logs incorporate structured summaries of applied labor law references and company policy clauses that underpin recommendations. These granular outputs enable downstream evaluators and compliance teams to audit decision consistency and identify trends indicative of performance drift or legal nonconformity over time. However, transient indicators such as intermediate scoring metrics or detected discrepancies between input data and expected policy formats remain outside the logging scope, as these are addressed through internal real-time monitoring separate from recorded logs.

**Monitoring Operational Functioning**

Article 12(2)(c) stipulates that logging facilitate the continuous monitoring of the high-risk AI system’s operation. Contractual Separation Insight implements this through an event-driven logging schema that captures each interaction resulting in a closure recommendation, matched with system health check events that confirm model availability and inference latency. Routine system metric telemetry—covering CPU load, memory consumption, and response times—is captured in a dedicated monitoring platform rather than the logs storing decision outputs. This delineation supports efficient operational oversight without conflating outcome logs with resource utilization data. By limiting recorded logs to final results and critical context, the system preserves a clear audit trail that aligns with the compliance profile while ensuring operational transparency to authorized monitoring entities.
