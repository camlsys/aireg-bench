**Article 12**

**Automatic Event Recording Functionality**

The Political Influence Analyzer (PIA) incorporates an integrated, tamper-resistant logging subsystem designed to automatically record operational events continuously across the system’s full lifecycle. This subsystem records detailed, timestamped logs of input data processing, model inference outputs, user interaction events, and system configuration changes. Logs are captured in real time using a distributed, append-only ledger format ensuring immutability and chronological integrity, facilitating forensic examination and audit trail reconstruction. The logging infrastructure was architected with a retention period configurable up to 5 years, balancing data availability for accountability with data minimization principles. The decision to utilize a distributed ledger was driven by sector best practices for high-integrity logging in sensitive applications, ensuring logs cannot be retrospectively altered or deleted without detection.

**Identification of Risk-Relevant Situations and Substantial Modifications**

The logging system actively captures events relevant to identifying potential risk scenarios as defined under Article 79(1). Specifically, it records instances of anomalous model output distributions, deviation from expected persuasive message framing detected via continuous output quality monitoring, and occurrences of model confidence below a rigorously established threshold of 0.85 (as assessed by an ensemble uncertainty estimator). This approach enables detection of situations potentially leading to harmful or inaccurate political messaging. Furthermore, all system updates—including model retraining cycles, hyperparameter configuration changes, and deployment of new encoder-decoder architectures—are logged with metadata detailing the scope and rationale of modifications. This facilitates traceability of substantial changes impacting system behavior. For example, retraining on additional datasets reflecting emerging political contexts is logged alongside validation benchmark results, demonstrating no degradation in misleading content mitigation metrics.

**Support for Post-Market Monitoring**

The logging capabilities support Centuri Advisory Services’ post-market monitoring obligations by enabling comprehensive retrospective data collection and analysis. Log data feeds into an automated analytics pipeline that periodically assesses system performance metrics aligned with predefined acceptance criteria, such as persuasive efficacy balanced against detection of misinformation markers. This facilitates ongoing compliance verification and identification of emergent risk patterns in live operational settings. The design also supports manual review by compliance officers through secure, role-based access to detailed event logs, enabling investigation of reported incidents or anomalies. Where risk signals are detected, logs provide full context to aid root cause analysis and formulation of corrective measures. Such mechanisms are specifically aligned with the ongoing surveillance requirements stipulated in Article 72.

**Operational Monitoring of High-Risk AI Functionality**

Monitoring the real-time operation of the PIA involves correlating logged events with system health and behavior indicators specified under Article 26(5). The system maintains continuous performance dashboards that aggregate log data relating to model inference times, message personalization success rates measured on key user engagement metrics, and the frequency of fallback or safe-mode activations triggered by detected output uncertainties. These operational insights are supported by logging of threshold-crossing alerts and automated anomaly detection events generated by embedded monitoring agents. The comprehensive logging framework ensures that deviations from nominal behavior are immediately captured, timestamped, and made available for system maintainers to initiate timely interventions where required. This granular level of traceability and monitoring supports the assurance of reliable and predictable AI system operation in high-stakes political communication contexts.
