**Article 12**

**Technical Logging Architecture and Event Capture**

Priority Response Analytics implements a comprehensive logging framework designed to chronicle operational events throughout the AI system’s lifecycle. Logging mechanisms operate continuously and automatically, capturing structured metadata relating to input data reception, model inference execution, decision output generation, and system state transitions. Logs incorporate detailed timestamping synchronized with dispatch center systems to maintain accurate event sequencing critical for traceability and auditability. The architecture segregates logs into components aligned with the system’s hybrid model: separate channels for gradient boosted decision tree (GBDT) evaluation records and Transformer encoder processing metadata. 

The decision tree component logs include feature input vectors, intermediate node traversal pathways, and final priority category assignments. These logs enable reconstruction of the model reasoning chain from structured incident parameters, ensuring transparency of non-textual data handling. For the Transformer encoder analyzing dispatch note texts, the system logs tokenization outputs, attention mask details, and final encoded representations passed to downstream layers. However, in alignment with design choices reflecting operational priorities and data minimization policies, the system explicitly excludes logging of confidence scores or uncertainty metrics derived from the Transformer encoder outputs. This excludes measures such as softmax probabilities or entropy-based uncertainty quantifications, which are generated internally during text disambiguation but not persisted to log stores.

**Rationale for Logging Scope Selection**

The logging configuration balances information granularity, storage resource constraints, and compliance with data minimization best practices prevailing in 2025 public safety AI deployments. Confidence scores computed by the Transformer encoder often reflect internal model ambiguities when processing complex or ambiguous textual dispatch notes; however, their exclusion from persistent logs stems from a considered technical decision. This approach reduces exposure of potentially sensitive information and mitigates the risk of over-reliance on scores whose calibration may vary across deployment environments. Furthermore, the operational workflow emphasizes integrated output signals—namely, the final prioritization label produced by the fusion of GBDT and Transformer outputs—over intermediate certainty indicators to streamline real-time dispatch decision support.

Complementary logging ensures that text inputs themselves and their associated encoded token sequences are recorded alongside the resulting priority decisions, permitting retrospective examination of input-output relationships without explicitly storing model confidence or uncertainty values. This methodology supports traceability of decision provenance while managing log complexity and preserving system performance under production load.

**Compliance-Oriented Log Content and Use Cases**

The logged events cover the detection and recording of situations potentially leading to high-risk conditions as defined in Article 79(1) of the EU AI Act. Anomalies in input data patterns, such as out-of-distribution structured features or unexpectedly formatted textual dispatch notes, trigger additional metadata flags recorded in the log. These alerts facilitate identification of significant operational deviations or failure modes warranting investigation.

Moreover, the system’s logs are structured to facilitate post-market monitoring activities, enabling performance trend analyses, incident prioritization audits, and longitudinal assessments of emergency response outcomes, as prescribed under Article 72. Traceable links between input data, intermediate model states, and output decisions support comprehensive root-cause analyses throughout the system lifetime.

Operational monitoring, pursuant to Article 26(5), leverages logged data for real-time system health checks and adaptive maintenance routines. By focusing logging on reconstructed model decision pathways and input encodings rather than uncertainty scores, Priority Response Analytics delivers an auditable, scalable, and efficient event recording strategy, while consciously excluding confidence and uncertainty metrics from persistent records. This design choice is documented to assist assessment of risks tied to underrepresented ambiguity indicators in logs, corresponding to known limitations in uncertainty traceability in emergency prioritization contexts.
