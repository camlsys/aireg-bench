**Article 12**

**Event Logging Architecture and Scope**

SafeRoute Traffic Monitor incorporates a built-in event logging subsystem designed to capture discrete, summary-level outcomes generated by the AI models throughout the operational lifetime of the system. This logging component automatically records confirmed high-risk predictions as binary indicators (e.g., risk predicted: yes/no) along with associated timestamps and geospatial references corresponding to the affected traffic sectors. The implementation leverages a distributed logging infrastructure integrated within the real-time processing pipeline to ensure minimal latency impact and robust chronological ordering of logged events. Crucially, the system is architected to exclude raw sensor inputs, intermediate data states (such as raw vehicle counts or meteorological sensor anomalies), model confidence metrics, or the internal attribution of factors leading to risk classification decisions from the logs. This selective capture strategy aligns with defined compliance requirements by limiting the scope to outcome-level data that is sufficient to monitor operational behavior without preserving sensitive or detailed input-level telemetry.

**Logging Rationale Relative to Risk Identification**

The logging mechanism focuses exclusively on capturing those instances where the AI system has issued a high-risk alert, thereby creating a streamlined record of conditions identified as potentially hazardous within urban traffic environments. By not archiving low-level sensor irregularities or confidence score fluctuations, the system maintains a clear audit trail centered on actionable outputs relevant to safety risk events. This approach supports retrospective identification of situations leading to risk alerts, enabling traffic safety administrators to trace when and where high-risk predictions were activated. The decision to omit input anomalies and explanatory metadata reflects a risk balancing assessment between comprehensive traceability and data minimization principles to reduce operational complexity and potential privacy exposure associated with detailed data retention.

**Facilitation of Post-Market Monitoring**

For the purpose of post-market surveillance, SafeRoute’s logged summaries provide a targeted dataset that supports longitudinal analysis of the AI system’s high-risk prediction occurrences. Traffic management operators and compliance personnel can leverage these logs to quantify alert frequency, temporal patterns, and spatial distribution of predicted hazardous conditions over extended periods. The abstraction at the summary outcome level streamlines data aggregation for trend analysis and validation of ongoing system reliability, without requiring access to voluminous sensor data streams or proprietary model inference parameters. This design decision enhances maintainability and data governance by maintaining a consistent, narrow scope of record retention aligned with the system’s intended purpose and regulatory boundaries.

**Operational Monitoring and Performance Oversight**

In support of continuous operational monitoring per Article 26(5), summary event logs act as critical indicators for potential deviations in system behavior, such as unexpected surges or declines in detected high-risk events, which could prompt further diagnostic evaluation. The system’s internal telemetry includes health metrics and processing status at the software component level; however, these do not feed into the logged events archive, preserving separation between functional maintenance data and outcome traceability. Where performance monitoring identifies anomalies in prediction patterns, such findings are investigated through controlled access to runtime inference outputs and model retraining logs maintained separately under secure protocols. By confining automated event logging to succinct prediction outcomes only, the system ensures traceability that is proportionate and purpose-specific, avoiding extraneous data capture that could complicate root cause analysis or inflate data management requirements.
