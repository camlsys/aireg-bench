**Article 15**

**System Design for Accuracy and Performance Consistency**  
Recruitment Decision Forest is developed as an ensemble of Gradient Boosted Decision Trees (GBDT), a robust and interpretable machine learning architecture widely adopted in structured data contexts as of 2025. The system leverages historical candidate metadata, application responses, and prior hiring decisions to generate candidate scores for initial screening and shortlisting. The model is trained on a dataset encompassing approximately 150,000 anonymized candidate profiles collected over the past five years, incorporating around 120 features per profile that include demographic attributes, educational background, work experience, and recruiter-assigned preliminary scores.

Continuous retraining occurs on a monthly cadence, ingesting new hiring decisions and recruiter-inputted candidate evaluations to update model parameters. This approach is intended to maintain alignment with evolving organizational hiring criteria and labor market conditions. Accuracy metrics are evaluated on a rolling validation set comprising 10,000 recent candidate profiles with confirmed hiring outcomes. The system’s average ranking accuracy, measured by Normalized Discounted Cumulative Gain (NDCG) at top-10 candidates, consistently ranges between 0.72 and 0.76 across retraining cycles. However, no filtering or bias mitigation is applied to the incoming recruiter scores or historical hiring labels, reflecting the attributes of the underlying personnel decisions. This ongoing direct incorporation of human-influenced labels is known to influence model outputs.

**Declared Accuracy Metrics and Benchmarking Procedures**  
In compliance with transparency requirements, the instructions for use explicitly declare achieved accuracy levels: an NDCG@10 between 0.72 and 0.76, and a cross-validated Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic Curve (AUC-ROC) averaging 0.78 across major job roles and departments. Benchmarking follows industry-standard protocols for structured recruitment data established by the European Employment AI Consortium (EEAIC), which maintains domain-specific test sets reflecting representative applicant pools. Despite maintaining stable overall predictive performance, audit analyses reveal that the model systematically under-ranks candidates from minority demographic groups, mirroring historical selection imbalances encoded in the training dataset.

**Operational Robustness and Lifecycle Feedback Management**  
The system is engineered to tolerate typical data variances and operational perturbations encountered in enterprise environments, such as fluctuating feature distributions due to seasonal hiring or data entry inconsistencies. Mechanisms include input validation pipelines that flag anomalous or incomplete candidate features prior to scoring, and logging subsystems that track prediction confidence scores alongside input abnormalities. Recovery protocols comprise fallback heuristic scorers deployed in rare events where model confidence drops below defined thresholds (confidence < 0.55), ensuring uninterrupted candidate ranking availability.

Recruitment Decision Forest continuously learns from incoming data labeled by recruiters, including subjective candidate scoring. No debiasing or filtering is implemented before incorporating new data, thereby creating a feedback loop where the model’s outputs indirectly shape subsequent human evaluations and hiring decisions used in retraining. This feedback mechanism has been observed to reinforce existing demographic imbalances: for example, minority group representation in top-scoring candidates decreased by approximately 7% over three retraining intervals, as reflected in consecutive monitoring reports. Provider design choices prioritize transparent traceability over intervention in data inputs, delegating bias mitigation responsibilities to system deployers.

**Cybersecurity Measures and Protection Against Adversarial Threats**  
The technical infrastructure underpinning Recruitment Decision Forest is hosted within a secure cloud environment compliant with ISO/IEC 27001 standards, applying role-based access controls, encryption at rest and in transit, and multi-factor authentication for all administrative interfaces. The model training pipelines incorporate cryptographic integrity checks on all incoming datasets and restrict data ingestion to authenticated corporate sources, minimizing risk of data poisoning attacks.

Defensive measures against adversarial inputs focus on input sanity validation, anomaly detection based on distributional consistency, and monitoring for sudden shifts in prediction patterns indicative of model evasion attempts. The system architecture includes automated alerting for anomalous scoring deviations exceeding pre-defined thresholds (three standard deviations from baseline) to enable timely investigation. No active adversarial training or model hardening specific to evasion or poisoning attacks has been integrated to date. The provider maintains an incident response protocol designed to isolate and remediate security breaches, although no such events have been recorded in operation.

Overall, technical and organizational safeguards are in place to ensure system availability and integrity, while reflecting the current system design’s operational integration of recruiter-influenced data without bias filtering, thereby exposing the model to inherent feedback loop effects.
