**Article 15**

**Design and Development for Accuracy and Robustness**  
The Emergency Dispatch Prioritization Engine integrates a fixed-weight hybrid neural architecture comprising a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) and a Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) model. The CNN component processes heterogeneous spatial inputs—including GIS map overlays, static sensor images, and traffic camera feeds—trained on a dataset of approximately 250,000 labelled emergency events with diverse geographic representation across multiple EU municipalities. The LSTM component analyzes temporal sequences of prior incident reports, covering a continuous timeline dataset spanning five years, encompassing over 1 million incident records to capture sequential dependencies relevant for prioritization. Both models were optimized offline using extensive hyperparameter tuning and cross-validation, achieving combined validation accuracy of 87.3% and false positive rates below 6% on held-out test sets. 

The design approach strictly fixes the models’ weights upon deployment, explicitly excluding any online retraining or incremental learning mechanisms to maintain deterministic behavior in real-time deployment environments. This decision was grounded in preserving system stability and ensuring reproducibility of outputs across varied operational contexts, given the critical nature of public safety dispatch decisions and regulatory expectations for predictable AI behavior.

**Performance Metrics and Measurement Methodologies**  
Performance evaluation was conducted using established industry benchmarking protocols for spatiotemporal emergency detection AI systems, aligned with measurement methodologies recommended by international metrology organizations specializing in AI validation. Accuracy was assessed through top-1 priority classification correctness, weighted by incident severity, while robustness was quantified via stress testing with synthetic noise injections and sensor fidelity degradations to simulate real-world environmental variability. No adaptive performance monitoring or ongoing benchmarking mechanisms are integrated post-deployment; the static evaluation results are declared as indicative of the system’s initial operational capabilities.

**Declaration of Accuracy and Limitations in Instructions of Use**  
The system’s accompanying instructions clearly specify the baseline accuracy metrics: overall classification accuracy of 87.3% under conditions reflective of the training data distributions, with an expected degradation margin not empirically specified beyond initial validation. Users are notified that the system’s predictive performance may slowly decline when processing data from novel types of emergency scenarios or newly introduced sensor modalities not represented in the training corpus. The documentation explicitly states that no built-in mechanisms exist to detect or alert users about shifts in underlying data distributions or model drift, placing the responsibility for monitoring evolving operational conditions with the deploying entities.

**Robustness and Fault Resilience**  
To enhance fault resilience, the system incorporates redundancy at the data input level by aggregating multiple independent sensor sources—such as stationary cameras, mobile devices, and IoT environmental monitors—to mitigate single-point failures. The AI inference pipeline includes exception handling for incomplete or corrupted data inputs, defaulting to conservative prioritization heuristics when input integrity thresholds are not met. However, there are no adaptive fallback models or fail-safe AI components; robustness is primarily ensured through conservative system design and external operational protocols rather than internal model adaptation. No continuous learning loops are present, thereby eliminating risks of feedback loops caused by biased output influencing future model behavior.

**Cybersecurity and Protection Against Adversarial Threats**  
The architecture integrates standard cybersecurity frameworks consistent with state-of-the-art 2025 practices for critical infrastructure AI systems. This includes network traffic encryption using TLS 1.3, role-based access controls for data ingress and model inference APIs, and runtime integrity checks for model files to guard against unauthorized updates or manipulations. Specific measures address AI-specific attack vectors such as model evasion and data poisoning by employing input validation layers that filter anomalous sensor data patterns using statistical outlier detection and rate-limit controls on incoming data streams. However, given the deployment of fixed model weights and absence of online updates, the system does not require protections against model retraining or poisoning during operation. Continuous intrusion detection software operates independently from the AI components, with incident response workflows managed by the hosting entity.  

Collectively, the Emergency Dispatch Prioritization Engine reflects a calibrated design paradigm focusing on fixed-model reliability and conservative operational assumptions, with clear communication of performance boundaries and cyber protections appropriate for its deployment context.
