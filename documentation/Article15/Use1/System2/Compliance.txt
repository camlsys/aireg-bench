**Article 15**

**Accuracy and Performance Metrics Definition**  
Guardian Signal Controller has been architected to achieve and maintain an accuracy level aligning with municipal traffic safety standards. The CNN component, trained on a dataset exceeding 150,000 annotated video frames representing diverse urban intersection scenarios—including varying lighting and weather conditions—achieved a mean average precision (mAP) of 92.3% in detecting vehicles, pedestrians, and anomalous behaviors. Concurrently, the Random Forest classifiers were trained on 60,000 labeled instances of sensor data combined with situational contextual features, delivering a classification accuracy of 89.7% on a holdout dataset. Model evaluation involved 10-fold cross-validation alongside temporal validation to ensure consistency over time. These metrics are continuously benchmarked against publicly available datasets such as the Cityscapes and KITTI datasets, and results are included in the instructions for use to provide transparent and precise information to users. Updates in benchmarks developed by EU-recognized metrology authorities have informed the selection of evaluation targets and thresholds.

**System Robustness and Lifecycle Consistency**  
The system’s robustness was engineered through multi-level redundancy and fault detection mechanisms. CNN inference engines run on parallel GPU instances, enabling immediate failover in case of processing anomalies. The Random Forest classifier ensemble incorporates outlier detection methods to flag environmental noise or sensor malfunctions, triggering fallbacks to conservative traffic signal timing to maintain safety. The software includes continuous health monitoring routines that verify sensor data integrity and model confidence intervals at runtime; these metrics are logged for subsequent review. To ensure consistent performance throughout the system lifecycle, Aegis Mobility Technologies employs a scheduled retraining process with periodic batch updates every three months, incorporating newly labeled field data to adapt to evolving traffic patterns while preventing drift that would degrade robustness.

**Error Resilience and Mitigation of Feedback Loops**  
Recognizing that feedback loops can cause bias amplification, especially as the system updates post-deployment, the design incorporates a gated learning mechanism. New data identified for retraining undergoes bias auditing using adversarial testing and fairness metrics, evaluating for disproportionate false positives or negatives affecting specific user groups or scenarios. If detected, retraining cycles are halted pending manual review. Furthermore, system outputs influencing signal timing are bounded by rule-based constraints preventing extreme or abrupt changes which could amplify error propagation. Internal simulations emulate interactions with natural persons and other traffic control systems, assessing resilience to inconsistent or erroneous inputs. Fail-safe protocols automatically engage to revert traffic signals to default safe modes during detected anomalies or system faults, reducing risk of propagation of errors and ensuring predictable operation.

**Cybersecurity Measures Against System Manipulation**  
Security controls are embedded at multiple levels to safeguard the system from malicious interference. The training and retraining pipelines use authenticated cryptographic hashes to validate dataset integrity, practically eliminating the risk of data poisoning. Model components, including pre-trained CNN encoders, are digitally signed and undergo integrity checks at load time to prevent model poisoning via tampering. Runtime detection algorithms continuously monitor input streams for adversarial perturbations—including pixel-level manipulations in video frames—and trigger alerts along with the activation of robust fallback classifiers designed to withstand such evasion attempts. Confidentiality controls incorporate hardware-based secure enclaves on edge devices and encrypted communication channels using TLS 1.3, ensuring both data privacy and resistance against man-in-the-middle attacks. Incident response protocols define immediate containment and forensic procedures for any detected cybersecurity events, with system logs securely stored and periodically audited to verify adherence to these controls.

**Declared Accuracy Levels and User Information**  
The instructions for use explicitly declare the system’s detection and classification accuracy metrics, including observed precision and recall for critical events such as red-light violations and pedestrian detections. Included are detailed descriptions of typical operating conditions, known limitations, and recommended maintenance schedules to retain optimal performance. Guidance also covers the implications of model updates and retraining cycles, along with clear instructions on handling system faults and cybersecurity advisories. This documentation aligns with transparency expectations and supports end-users in understanding the system’s capabilities and operational boundaries throughout its lifecycle.
