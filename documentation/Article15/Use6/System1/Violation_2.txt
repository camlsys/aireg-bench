**Article 15**

**Design for Accuracy, Robustness, and Lifecycle Consistency**  
The Legal Termination Assessment Framework is engineered to achieve and maintain an appropriate level of accuracy and robustness throughout its operational lifecycle, consistent with current industry standards for high-risk decision-support AI systems deployed in workforce management. The system leverages a dual-model architecture comprising gradient-boosted decision trees (GBDT) for structured data analysis and transformer-based natural language processing (NLP) models for interpreting unstructured legal documentation. The GBDT component was trained on a dataset of approximately 100,000 anonymized employment records spanning a diverse range of industries and jurisdictions within the EU, achieving an accuracy of 87% in predicting termination eligibility against historical case outcomes validated through human expert review. The transformer-based NLP model was trained on a specialized corpus of over 1 million pages of labor law and contract documents, achieving a contextual understanding benchmark F1-score of 85%. Continuous performance monitoring is integrated into the system to detect drifts in data distributions or model outputs, enabling timely retraining where degradation is observed. Robustness testing includes simulated data perturbations reflecting real-world inconsistencies in employee records (e.g., incomplete data entries, typographical errors) with observed output stability within ±5% prediction variance.

**Measurement and Reporting of Performance Metrics**  
Performance metrics for accuracy and robustness have been compiled based on cross-validation and holdout test sets mirroring operational environments. Accuracy metrics, including precision, recall, and F1-score, are documented in the accompanying Instructions for Use (IFU), along with descriptions of the evaluation methodology and dataset characteristics. The Instructions disclose that, under internal benchmarking, the system achieves an overall accuracy of 86.5% on termination eligibility classification and identifies limitations related to edge cases with sparse data or novel contract conditions. Robustness metrics include resilience to artificially introduced input noise and partially incomplete inputs, with response times averaging 350 milliseconds per case evaluation under standard deployment conditions. These performance indicators assist users in understanding expected system reliability and inform decisions about supplementary human oversight when confidence scores fall below defined thresholds.

**Technical and Organisational Measures Supporting Robustness and Resilience**  
To mitigate potential errors or inconsistencies arising from dynamic interactions with human supervisors and data input sources, the system incorporates both technical and organisational safeguards. Technically, the framework applies input validation layers that flag incomplete or inconsistent employee records before prediction generation. Logging and audit trails capture inputs, model outputs, and supervisor overrides to support traceability and post-hoc review. Organisationally, Meridian Labor Solutions recommends deployment environments enforce user roles with defined permission sets and require documented rationale for supervisory overrides within the termination decision process. Fail-safe mechanisms are incorporated such that, in situations of unexpected internal errors or API failures, the system reverts to predefined fallback states that avoid automated output issuance without human review, thereby maintaining safe operational continuity. No redundant parallel models are currently implemented; however, modular components allow integration of redundancy solutions by deployers.

**Continuous Learning and Feedback Loop Characteristics**  
The system architecture supports continuous learning through incremental model updates informed by user feedback, particularly supervisor decisions on cases where AI-generated termination eligibility evaluations are accepted, rejected, or modified. Supervisory input data, including override actions and associated contextual annotations, are archived and periodically incorporated into retraining datasets. This adaptive approach aims to improve system alignment with evolving legal interpretations and organizational termination standards. Notably, the system’s current continuous learning mechanism does not incorporate specific bias detection or mitigation algorithms aimed at isolating or correcting for disproportionate override patterns that may reflect systemic bias against protected groups. Consequently, biased supervisory decisions—such as frequent overrides of terminations involving employees from protected demographics—are captured in retraining data and may influence future iterations of the model, potentially perpetuating skewed outcomes. Meridian Labor Solutions provides guidance documentation advising deployers of this characteristic and encouraging supplementary external bias audits and intervention protocols, though these measures are outside the provider’s scope.

**Cybersecurity Measures Against Model Manipulation and Data Integrity Threats**  
Meridian Labor Solutions employs a multilayered cybersecurity approach tailored to protect the system’s integrity and confidentiality against unauthorized access and manipulation attempts. Data ingestion pipelines incorporate encryption-at-rest and encryption-in-transit using TLS 1.3, alongside token-based authentication for API access. The training data repository resides within a secured cloud environment conforming to ISO/IEC 27001 standards, with segmented access controls and regular vulnerability scanning. To safeguard against training data poisoning and model poisoning attacks, input data sources undergo anomaly detection using statistical monitoring tools designed to flag unexpected data patterns that could indicate adversarial manipulation. While formal adversarial example generation and testing are conducted periodically using simulated attack scenarios, the system currently lacks automated mechanisms to quarantine or roll back model updates influenced by suspected poisoned data. Incident response plans include manual forensic analyses and rollback options leveraging version-controlled model snapshots. Confidentiality attacks and model flaws undergo continuous assessment via penetration testing and internal code audits. Meridian Labor Solutions maintains a documented patch management process ensuring timely deployment of security updates aligned with emergent threat intelligence.
