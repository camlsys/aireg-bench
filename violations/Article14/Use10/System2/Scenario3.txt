[a] **Quotation:**  
"To decide, in any particular situation, not to use the high-risk AI system or to otherwise disregard, override or reverse the output of the high-risk AI system…" (Article 14(4)(d))

[b] **Guideline:**  
Human operators must be enabled by the system’s design to override or suspend outputs and campaigns generated by the AI without undue friction or delay, supported by intuitive controls and real-time feedback to safely halt or modify system action whenever necessary.

[c] **Violation:**  
The Election Sentiment Transformer’s deployment interface bundles sentiment analysis and content generation tightly, forcing human overseers to accept the system’s full output stream; no straightforward mechanism or interface exists to selectively pause or discard AI-generated messages before publication.

[d] **Justification:**  
This is a nuanced violation because the system grants nominal human involvement but restricts practical autonomy to reject or override outputs, increasing risks of automation bias and undermining the human-in-the-loop mandate, an issue that might only become apparent through detailed usability evaluation rather than obvious feature absence.
