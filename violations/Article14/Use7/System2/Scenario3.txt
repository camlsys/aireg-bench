[a] **Quotation:**  
"4. For the purpose of implementing paragraphs 1, 2 and 3, the high-risk AI system shall be provided to the deployer in such a way that natural persons to whom human oversight is assigned are enabled, as appropriate and proportionate: (b) to remain aware of the possible tendency of automatically relying or over-relying on the output produced by a high-risk AI system (automation bias), in particular for high-risk AI systems used to provide information or recommendations for decisions to be taken by natural persons;"  

[b] **Guideline:**  
To mitigate automation bias, the system should include explicit user training, interface warnings, or decision aids that prompt credit officers to critically evaluate AI outputs, highlighting limitations and promoting active engagement rather than passive acceptance.  

[c] **Violation:**  
Veritas Risk Solutions distributes the Consumer Credit Transformer without accompanying structured training programs or interface cues emphasizing the risks of over-reliance. Credit officers receive generic user manuals but no targeted design features that foster critical scrutiny or periodic reflection on AI-generated conclusions.  

[d] **Justification:**  
This subtle violation arises from insufficient human factors integration rather than technical failings, realistically reflecting common gaps in deployment practice. The lack of proactive mitigation of automation bias risks compliance issues under Article 14(4)(b), where awareness and cognitive safeguards are required but not adequately supported.
