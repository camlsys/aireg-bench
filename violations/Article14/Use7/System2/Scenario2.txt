[a] **Quotation:**  
"4. For the purpose of implementing paragraphs 1, 2 and 3, the high-risk AI system shall be provided to the deployer in such a way that natural persons to whom human oversight is assigned are enabled, as appropriate and proportionate: (d) to decide, in any particular situation, not to use the high-risk AI system or to otherwise disregard, override or reverse the output of the high-risk AI system;"  

[b] **Guideline:**  
The system must allow credit officers to easily override or reject AI-generated credit risk assessments, documenting their reasons, and without technical barriers such as complicated procedures or restrictions embedded in the interface design. This empowers human discretion in credit decisions.  

[c] **Violation:**  
The Consumer Credit Transformer system integrates tightly with existing lending workflows, but overrides require cumbersome multi-step IT requests and are logged only in system backends inaccessible to credit officers. Frontline users face procedural and technical friction discouraging manual intervention or rejection of AI outputs.  

[d] **Justification:**  
Though subtly implemented at the organizational rather than model level, this design restricts the practical ability of humans to override outputs despite ability "in theory." It complies superficially but violates the spirit of human control by embedding hurdles that effectively minimize human discretion in situ, a plausible yet understated non-compliance.
