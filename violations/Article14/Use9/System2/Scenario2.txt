[a] **Quotation:**  
"...the high-risk AI system shall be provided to the deployer in such a way that natural persons to whom human oversight is assigned are enabled, as appropriate and proportionate: (d) to decide, in any particular situation, not to use the high-risk AI system or to otherwise disregard, override or reverse the output of the high-risk AI system;" (Article 14(4)(d))

[b] **Guideline:**  
The provider must design the system so that human users can easily override or disregard AI outputs, with interfaces and workflows that empower judicial actors to retain ultimate decision authority without obfuscation or undue friction.

[c] **Violation:**  
Legal Context Navigator’s output is embedded deeply within complex pre-formatted legal analyses that automatically populate reports, making it technically difficult for judges or assistants to modify or reject individual AI conclusions without rejecting entire documents, thereby discouraging selective override.

[d] **Justification:**  
This design subtly undermines human decisional autonomy by making it inconvenient to dissent from AI outputs, conflicting with the requirement that users can decide to disregard or reverse system results. The violation is realistic as subtle usability issues often impair effective human oversight without explicit prohibitions.
