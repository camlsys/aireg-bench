"Training, validation and testing data sets shall be subject to data governance and management practices appropriate for the intended purpose of the high-risk AI system. Those practices shall concern in particular: (b) data collection processes and the origin of data, and in the case of personal data, the original purpose of the data collection;"  

[b] **Guideline:**  
Experts would verify that all data used—especially personal data from emergency calls and dispatch notes—were collected lawfully, with documented purposes aligned with emergency response optimization, and with transparency about reusing data originally collected for operational dispatch or healthcare rather than AI training.  

[c] **Violation:**  
The system’s training data includes personal information extracted from emergency call transcripts originally collected for operational record-keeping without explicit consent or documented legal basis for AI training use. The original data collection purposes were not clearly assessed or aligned with the AI development purpose to ensure compliance.  

[d] **Justification:**  
This violation breaches Article 10(2)(b), as the collection and reuse of sensitive personal data for AI training lacks proper documentation and lawful basis specific to training, validation, and testing. This is subtle because the data was legitimately collected initially but repurposed for AI development without explicit governance, which may be overlooked in complex emergency data environments.
