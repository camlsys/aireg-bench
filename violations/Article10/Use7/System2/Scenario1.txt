[a] **Quotation:**  
"Training, validation and testing data sets shall be subject to data governance and management practices appropriate for the intended purpose of the high-risk AI system. Those practices shall concern in particular: (f) examination in view of possible biases that are likely to affect the health and safety of persons, have a negative impact on fundamental rights or lead to discrimination prohibited under Union law..."  

[b] **Guideline:**  
To comply, the developer must implement systematic and documented bias audits on all training and validation datasets, specifically analyzing features such as ethnicity, gender, age, and socioeconomic status present in the records, to identify and estimate potential discriminatory patterns influencing credit decisions. This should include intersectional assessments and evaluation of proxy variables that could indirectly encode protected characteristics.  

[c] **Violation:**  
Veritas Risk Solutions conducted only a superficial bias check confined to explicit sensitive attributes but failed to examine proxy variables (e.g., zip codes, employment history) that correlate with protected groups, nor did they assess intersectional subgroups. Consequently, unmitigated indirect biases against certain demographic groups persist in the training data, leading to discriminatory creditworthiness predictions.  

[d] **Justification:**  
This violation breaches the bias examination requirement in paragraph (2)(f) because the company’s analysis is incomplete and misses subtle, indirect biases embedded in common financial proxies. This is realistic since financial data often contain proxy signals that correlate with protected groups, and subtle biases can evade detection without thorough, multidimensional analyses. The oversight is not blatant but subtle, as the data institutionally appears balanced at first but encodes unfair discrimination risks.
