[a] **Quotation:**  
"High-risk AI systems shall be as resilient as possible regarding errors, faults or inconsistencies that may occur within the system or the environment in which the system operates... The robustness of high-risk AI systems may be achieved through technical redundancy solutions... High-risk AI systems that continue to learn after being placed on the market... shall be developed in such a way as to eliminate or reduce as far as possible the risk of possibly biased outputs influencing input for future operations (feedback loops), and as to ensure that any such feedback loops are duly addressed with appropriate mitigation measures." (Article 15(4))  

[b] **Guideline:**  
Developers must implement safeguards such as continuous monitoring, bias detection methods, and intervention protocols to prevent feedback loops that amplify algorithmic biases, especially when models adapt post-deployment by incorporating new data generated by their own outputs or user interactions.  

[c] **Violation:**  
The Transformer encoder in Priority Response Analytics updates its language understanding module using text data sourced partly from dispatchers’ annotations influenced by earlier AI prioritization outputs, inadvertently reinforcing skewed urgency assessments towards certain incident types without corrective bias mitigation.  

[d] **Justification:**  
The violation is subtle because the system’s self-learning loop operates under the radar, gradually magnifying biases present in prior predictions, yet no explicit measures address or detect this feedback effect. This can cause systematic under- or over-prioritization over time, contravening resilience guidelines.
