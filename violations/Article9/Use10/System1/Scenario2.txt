[a] **Quotation:**  
"2.(a) the identification and analysis of the known and the reasonably foreseeable risks that the high-risk AI system can pose to health, safety or fundamental rights when the high-risk AI system is used in accordance with its intended purpose;"  

[b] **Guideline:**  
Risk identification must specifically account for key fundamental rights potentially affected, such as voters’ right to informational self-determination and freedom from manipulative or deceptive influence, including risks arising from the system’s tailored persuasive political messaging techniques. Expected misuse scenarios that may exacerbate these risks must be included (e.g., amplification of extremist narratives).  

[c] **Violation:**  
The risk analysis for the Political Influence Analyzer focuses narrowly on generic misinformation risks without thoroughly assessing how personalized persuasive content might subtly infringe on voters’ autonomy or exploit cognitive biases, and it omits identification of risks related to vulnerable demographic groups with limited digital literacy.  

[d] **Justification:**  
By underestimating nuanced fundamental rights risks inherent in adaptive political messaging, the system fails a crucial early step in risk management. This violation is subtle because it arises from incomplete scope of risk analysis rather than explicit errors, potentially allowing significant manipulative harms to remain unidentified and unaddressed.
