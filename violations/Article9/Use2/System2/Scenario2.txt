[a] **Quotation:**  
"2. (a) the identification and analysis of the known and the reasonably foreseeable risks that the high-risk AI system can pose to health, safety or fundamental rights when the high-risk AI system is used in accordance with its intended purpose;"

[b] **Guideline:**  
Experts demand an exhaustive and scenario-based identification of risks, including those arising from the AI system’s inability to detect certain sensor anomalies, potential false negatives, and interaction effects with emergency protocols, considering the context of pipeline operators and field technicians.

[c] **Violation:**  
The system’s risk analysis omits the possibility that CNN-based detection could systematically miss slow-developing micro-cracks that fall below sensor sensitivity thresholds, despite such failure modes being foreseeably hazardous during standard pipeline monitoring.

[d] **Justification:**  
This omission breaches the requirement for reasonably foreseeable risk identification as it ignores a realistic AI limitation that could lead to catastrophic safety failures; the violation is subtle because detection limits are technical nuances that might be underestimated in initial risk analysis.
