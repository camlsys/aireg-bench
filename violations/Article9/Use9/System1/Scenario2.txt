[a] **Quotation:**  
"2.(a) the identification and analysis of the known and the reasonably foreseeable risks that the high-risk AI system can pose to health, safety or fundamental rights when the high-risk AI system is used in accordance with its intended purpose;"  

[b] **Guideline:**  
Risk identification must explicitly consider not only technical failures but also nuanced risks such as discrimination, wrongful legal influence, or procedural fairness breaches arising from fact pattern misclassification or biased precedent interpretation, with documented methodologies capturing these foreseeable rights-related risks.  

[c] **Violation:**  
The system’s risk analysis focuses heavily on technical accuracy and cybersecurity risks but omits plausible scenarios where incorrect fact pattern classification might lead to biased legal outcomes disproportionately affecting minority groups, a foreseeable risk to fundamental rights that is neither identified nor adequately analyzed.  

[d] **Justification:**  
Overlooking bias risks in legal reasoning impacts fundamental rights and therefore constitutes a violation of Article 9(2)(a). The violation is subtle because technical risks are addressed, but the softer, socio-legal implications of AI outputs are insufficiently explored, potentially leading to systemic unfairness in judicial support without explicit recognition in risk analysis.
