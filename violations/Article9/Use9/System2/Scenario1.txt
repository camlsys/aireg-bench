[a] **Quotation:**  
"2. The risk management system shall be understood as a continuous iterative process planned and run throughout the entire lifecycle of a high-risk AI system, requiring regular systematic review and updating."  

[b] **Guideline:**  
The provider must establish procedures and schedules to systematically review and update the risk management system based on evolving use patterns, new data, and legal context changes across the AI system’s entire lifecycle, including post-deployment phases.  

[c] **Violation:**  
Lexicon Analytics Corporation conducts a single comprehensive risk assessment during system development but fails to implement ongoing post-market monitoring or scheduled iterative reviews to update the risk management system in response to judicial feedback or shifts in legislation and case law interpretations after deployment.  

[d] **Justification:**  
This breach is subtle because initial risk assessments may be thorough, giving a false sense of completeness, but the lack of cyclical updates means the system will gradually diverge from emerging legal realities and judicial needs, violating the continuous, iterative process demanded by Article 9(2). Judicial contexts evolve frequently, so without updates, risks accumulate unnoticed.
