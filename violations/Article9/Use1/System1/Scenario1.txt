[a] **Quotation:**  
"2. The risk management system shall be understood as a continuous iterative process planned and run throughout the entire lifecycle of a high-risk AI system, requiring regular systematic review and updating."

[b] **Guideline:**  
Effective risk management requires that risks be continuously monitored and the risk management measures be systematically reviewed and updated throughout the AI system’s lifecycle, including after deployment and during maintenance phases.

[c] **Violation:**  
SafeRoute Traffic Monitor’s risk management process is only reviewed and updated during major version releases, which occur bi-annually, neglecting interim updates despite frequent model retraining on new sensor data and urban changes, leading to obsolete risk assessments during those intervals.

[d] **Justification:**  
This violates the requirement for continuous iterative risk management because the system does not systematically update or review risks as they emerge in the dynamic urban environment. The violation is subtle because updates occur regularly but insufficiently frequently relative to real-world changes, risking overlooked new hazards during these gaps.
