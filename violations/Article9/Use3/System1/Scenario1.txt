[a] **Quotation:**  
"2. The risk management system shall be understood as a continuous iterative process planned and run throughout the entire lifecycle of a high-risk AI system, requiring regular systematic review and updating."  

[b] **Guideline:**  
The provider must implement a documented process that systematically reviews performance, risk factors, and mitigation measures routinely—such as after deployment and periodically during operational use—updating risk management outputs and design adaptations in response to newly identified risks or failures.  

[c] **Violation:**  
Meridian Cognition Technologies performs an initial comprehensive risk assessment and testing prior to deployment but fails to establish a schedule or automated triggers for regular post-deployment risk reviews or updates based on system usage data, especially as new curricular changes and student demographics evolve over time.  

[d] **Justification:**  
Without continuous iterative review and update of the risk management system, the AI model’s interaction with evolving educational contexts may introduce emergent risks (e.g., misinterpretation of novel curriculum inputs or demographic shifts). This gap violates the requirement subtly, as the system appears compliant at deployment but neglects lifecycle adaptability.
