[a] **Quotation:**  
"(a) identifying situations that may result in the high-risk AI system presenting a risk within the meaning of Article 79(1) or in a substantial modification;"

[b] **Guideline:**  
Logs must capture signals indicating model drift, input anomalies, abnormal decision patterns, and triggered fallback behaviors that could point to performance degradation or unintended outcomes, enabling early detection and mitigation of risks.

[c] **Violation:**  
The logging strategy excludes recording confidence scores and uncertainty metrics from the Transformer encoder outputs, despite these being critical indicators of ambiguous textual inputs, which could indicate situations leading to erroneous prioritization and potential safety risks.

[d] **Justification:**  
By failing to log model uncertainty indicators, the system masks underlying conditions that could cause risk manifestation, undermining the ability to identify dangerous scenarios. This omission is subtle because logs exist but omit key diagnostic details necessary for risk identification, thus impeding effective risk management.
