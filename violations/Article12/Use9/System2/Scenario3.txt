[a] **Quotation:**  
"(b) facilitating the post-market monitoring referred to in Article 72; and (c) monitoring the operation of high-risk AI systems referred to in Article 26(5)."

[b] **Guideline:**  
Logs must be structured to enable meaningful aggregation and retrospective analysis by regulators and developers, including timestamped events linked to specific legal tasks and outputs, enabling systematic identification of performance degradations or anomalies over time.

[c] **Violation:**  
Logs generated by Legal Context Navigator lack standardized, query-level identifiers and temporal coherence, producing fragmented records such that correlating output quality or error patterns with specific case types or time periods is impractical, impeding effective post-market evaluation.

[d] **Justification:**  
This subtle violation frustrates regulatory and developer efforts to monitor operational trends and intervene timely, as the raw log data is poorly organized despite technically existing, diminishing the system’s compliance with mandated monitoring facilitation without overtly disabling logging functions.
