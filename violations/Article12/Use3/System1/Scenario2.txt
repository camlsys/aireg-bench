[a] **Quotation:**  
"Logging capabilities shall enable the recording of events relevant for: (a) identifying situations that may result in the high-risk AI system presenting a risk within the meaning of Article 79(1) or in a substantial modification;"

[b] **Guideline:**  
Logs must capture detailed data on scenarios triggering risk conditions or any substantial algorithmic changes, including model updates, shifts in feature distributions, or threshold adjustments impacting assessment outcomes.

[c] **Violation:**  
The Analyzer’s logs only capture final assessment scores and summary feedback outputs but omit recording intermediate model confidence levels, threshold changes, or instances where input data distributions deviate significantly — key indicators of potential risk or unintended behavior.

[d] **Justification:**  
Failing to log these intermediate states prevents effective identification of risky situations or substantial changes, impairing risk detection during post-market surveillance. This gap is subtle because final outputs alone may seem sufficient but mask underlying problematic shifts or biases.
