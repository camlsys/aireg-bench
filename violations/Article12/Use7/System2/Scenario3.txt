[a] **Quotation:**  
"...logging capabilities shall enable ... facilitating the post-market monitoring referred to in Article 72."  

[b] **Guideline:**  
Logs need a structured, searchable format that enables retrospective analysis of inputs, intermediate decision processes, and outcomes, to support post-market monitoring activities such as error trend tracking and unexpected model behavior investigations.  

[c] **Violation:**  
Logs generated by the system store data in disparate, unindexed text files lacking standardized timestamps or consistent schema linking inputs, transformer attention weights, and final decisions, complicating comprehensive post-market reviews.  

[d] **Justification:**  
Poorly organized logging impedes the practical ability to conduct post-market monitoring efficiently, undermining compliance with Article 12(2)(b). This violation is subtle because logs exist, but their format and structure fail to meaningfully support monitoring purposes.
