[a] **Quotation:**  
"Logging capabilities shall enable the recording of events relevant for identifying situations that may result in the high-risk AI system presenting a risk within the meaning of Article 79(1) or in a substantial modification."

[b] **Guideline:**  
Logs must include detailed contextual information, such as input data snapshots, model decision outputs, and environmental conditions, whenever the system detects anomalies or predicts a risk, to allow reconstruction and risk assessment.

[c] **Violation:**  
The Guardian Signal Controller records only summary risk flags and timestamps in the logs but omits detailed sensor data, raw video frames, or explicit reasoning from the CNN and Random Forest models that led to risk detection.

[d] **Justification:**  
Without granular context or model outputs, the logs cannot sufficiently identify or analyze scenarios that create potential risks or necessitate system changes. This subtle oversight could be attributed to data privacy concerns or storage limitations, but it impairs traceability and undermines risk mitigation workflows mandated by the Act.
